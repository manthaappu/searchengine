{{unreferenced|date=January 2008}} In the [[mathematics|mathematical]] subfield of [[graph theory]] a '''level structure''' of a [[graph (mathematics)|graph]] is a [[Partition of a set|partition]] of the [[Set (mathematics)|set]] of [[vertex (graph theory)|vertices]] into [[equivalence classes]] of vertices with the same [[distance (graph theory)|distance]] from a given root vertex. ==Definition== Given a [[connected graph]] ''G''=(''V'',''E'') with ''V'' the set of [[vertex (graph theory)|vertices]] and ''E'' the set of [[edge (graph theory)|edges]] with :\epsilon:V \to \mathbb{N} the [[eccentricity (graph theory)|eccentricity]] of a vertex, for a given vertex ''v'' The partition :V = \bigcup_{i=0}^{\epsilon(v)} L_i(v)=V with :L_0(v):={v} :L_i(v):=\mathrm{Adj}(L_{i-1}(x)) \setminus \bigcup_{j=0}^{i-1} L_j(x) \qquad \mbox{ , } i=1,2,\ldots \epsilon(v) is called a '''level structure''' of ''G'' with '''root''' ''v'' and '''depth''' ε(''v'').
