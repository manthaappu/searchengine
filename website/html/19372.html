'''''Irreducible Mind: Toward a Psychology for the 21st Century''''' is a 2007 book by Edward F. Kelly, Emily Williams Kelly, Adam Crabtree, Alan Gauld, Michael Grosso, and [[Bruce Greyson]].Edward F. Kelly, Emily Williams Kelly, Adam Crabtree, Alan Gauld, Michael Grosso, and Bruce Greyson. ''[http://books.google.com.au/books?id=2oV61CeDx-YC&printsec=frontcover&dq=%22irreducible+mind%22+journal+review&source=bl&ots=cuFgzaGB8f&sig=16SmQk5RjqHcbl_1fGmdbg3XH9U&hl=en&ei=BJh8TM6VE4yAvgOW9a2tAg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=3&ved=0CB0Q6AEwAg#v=onepage&q&f=false Irreducible Mind: Toward a Psychology for the 21st Century]'' Lanham, MD: Rowman & Littlefield, 2007, xxxi + 800 pp. ISBN 978-0-7425-4792-6 The book begins by presenting a brief overview of contemporary [[neuroscience]] followed by a summary of the approach to scientific [[psychology]] proposed by [[Frederic William Henry Myers]]. Myers (and [[William James]]) posited that a "true science of mind should seriously take into account all kinds of human experiences before prematurely accepting a theory of mind".Alexander Moreira-Almeida. [http://www.hoje.org.br/site/arq/artigos/Kelly_book_review_JNMD08.pdf Book Review: Irreducible Mind] ''[[The Journal of Nervous and Mental Disease]]'', Volume 196, Number 4, April 2008, pp. 345-346. The book presents empirical studies of phenomena related to [[psychosomatic medicine]], [[placebo effect]]s, [[near-death experience]]s, mystical experiences, and creative genius, to argue for a "strongly dualistic theory of mind and brain".Dominic W. Massaro. [http://ajp.press.illinois.edu/123/2/br_6.html Book Review: Irreducible Mind] ''[[American Journal of Psychology]]'', Volume 123, Number 2, Summer 2010. ''Irreducible Mind'' depicts the mind as an entity independent of the brain or body, with which it causally interacts and the death of which it survives. The book "challenges neuroscientific reductionism"Paul Marshall. [http://www.imprint.co.uk/pdf/14_11_books.pdf Book Review: Irreducible Mind] ''[[Journal of Consciousness Studies]]'', Vol. 14, No. 11, 2007, pp. 125-128. as it argues that properties of minds cannot be fully explained by those of brains. The authorship of the book is diverse, with authors coming from the United States, United Kingdom, and Canada. The book is interdisciplinary in that the authors also come from various fields of psychology, science studies, and psychical research. Lead author Edward F. Kelly is Professor of Research in the Division of Perceptual Studies at the [[University of Virginia]].[http://www.medicine.virginia.edu/clinical/departments/psychiatry/sections/cspp/dops/staff/edbio-page Edward Francis Kelly, Ph.D.] Division of Perceptual Studies, University of Virginia. ==See also== *[[The Conscious Mind]] ==References== {{Parapsychology}} [[Category:Medical books]] [[Category:2007 books]] [[Category:Psychology books]] [[simple:Irreducible Mind]]
