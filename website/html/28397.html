A '''parallax barrier''' is a device placed in front of an image source, such as a [[liquid crystal display]], to allow it to show a [[3D display|stereoscopic image]] without the need for the viewer to wear [[Stereoscopy#3D_glasses|3D glasses]]. Placed in front of the normal LCD, it consists of a layer of material with a series of precision slits, allowing each eye to see a different set of [[pixel]]s, so creating a sense of depth through [[parallax]] in an effect similar to what [[lenticular printing]] produces for printed products.{{cite web |url=http://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,1759,1386949,00.asp |title=Reviews by PC Magazine - Sharp Actius RD3D |publisher=www.pcmag.com |accessdate=2008-01-25 |date=2003 }}{{cite web |url=http://www.theregister.co.uk/2004/08/12/3d_illusion/ |title=The Register - Sharp's 3D LCD: how's that work, then? |publisher=www.theregister.co.uk |accessdate=2008-01-25 |date=2004 }} A disadvantage of the technology is that the viewer must be positioned in a well defined spot to experience the 3D effect.{{cite news |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/mobile/article/0,2763,855027,00.html |title=Guardian Unlimited - Special reports - The return of 3D |publisher=www.guardian.co.uk |accessdate=2008-01-25 |date=2002 | location=London | first=Ashley | last=Norris }} ==Applications== In addition to films and computer games, the technique has found uses in areas such as [[molecular modelling]]{{Citation needed|date=January 2011}} and [[airport security]].{{cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/technology/3772563.stm |title=BBC NEWS - Technology - Easy 3D X-rays for air security |publisher=news.bbc.co.uk |accessdate=2008-01-25 |date=2004 }} It is also being used for the navigation system in the 2010-model [[Range Rover]], allowing the driver to view (for example) GPS directions, while a passenger watches a movie, and is used in the upcoming [[Nintendo 3DS]] hand-held game console.{{cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/technology/10323971.stm |title=Nintendo unveils 3DS handheld games console |publisher=www.bbc.co.uk |accessdate=2010-06-17 |date=2010 }} ==References== {{Reflist}} ==External links== * [http://mrl.nyu.edu/~perlin/experiments/autostereo/ Principle of autostereo display] - Java applet illustrating the idea (by Ken Perlin) [[Category:3D imaging]] [[Category:3D computer graphics]] {{physics-stub}} [[it:Barriera di Parallasse]] [[ru:Параллакс барьер]]
