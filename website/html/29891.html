'''Prestressed structure''' is the one whose overall [[integrity]], [[stability]] and [[security]] depend, primarily, on a ''prestressing''. ''Prestressing'' means the intentional creation of permanent stresses in a structure for the purpose of improving its performance under various service conditions{{cite book|title= Design of Prestressed Concrete|author=Nilson, Arthur H.|date=1987|publisher=John Wiley & Sons|isbn=0471830720}}.[[Image:Roman Colosseum With Moon.jpg|thumb|1,600 x 1,200 px|right|Naturally precompressed exterior wall of [[Colosseum]], Rome]] There are the following basic types of prestressing: *Precompression (mostly, with the own weight of a structure) *[[Prestressed concrete|Pretensioning]] with high-strength embedded tendons *[[Prestressed concrete|Post-tensioning]] with high-strength bonded or unbonded tendons [[Image:ThreeTwrBrCenter.jpg|thumb|800 x 600 px|left|Cable-stayed prestressed concrete bridge over [[Yangtze river]]]]Today, the concept of prestressed structure is widely employed in design of [[building]]s, underground structures, TV towers, power stations, floating storage and offshore facilities, [[nuclear reactor]] vessels, and numerous kinds of [[bridge]] systems{{cite book|title= Prestressed Concrete|author=Nawy, Edward G.|date=1989|publisher=Prentice Hall|isbn=0136983758}}. The idea of ''prestressing'' was, apparently, familiar to the ancient Rome architects; the tall attic wall of the [[Colosseum]] works as a [[press]] for the wall piers beneath. Tensional prestress appears to be a common property of biological structures, such as cells and the musculoskeletal system, as a natural (law-based) solution that provides form, mechanical stability, and emergence and flow of stimuli/information.
