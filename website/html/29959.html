In the context of [[knowledge-based systems]], Newell (in 1982) proposed the following '''principle of rationality''': : "If an agent has knowledge that one of its actions will lead to one of its goals, then the agent will select that action." This principle is employed by agents at the [[knowledge level]] to move closer to a desired goal. ==See also== *[[Artificial intelligence]] *[[Knowledge level modeling]] *[[Rationality]] *[[Cognitive science]] ==References== [1] [[Allen Newell]].
