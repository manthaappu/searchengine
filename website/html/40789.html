XOS was an operating system for the [[Scientific Data Systems|XDS]] [[SDS Sigma 7|Sigma]] line of computers "optimized for direct replacement of IBM [[DOS/360 and successors|DOS/360]] installations" and to provide [[Real-time operating system|real-time]] and [[timesharing]] support [http://www.bitsavers.org/pdf/sds/sigma/xos/XOS_Fact_Sheet_Aug72.pdf "XOS Fact Sheet, 1972"]. XOS was developed, beginning in 1969, for Xerox by the French firm CII (now [[Groupe Bull|Bull]]).[http://books.google.com/books?id=b5for5RdBCoC&lpg=PA29&ots=zpzZ-lu3wo&dq=xds%20xos&pg=PA29#v=onepage&q=xds%20xos&f=false Strassmann, Paul A. (2008), ''The Computers Nobody Wanted: My Years with Xerox'']. Information Economics Press. XOS was more successful in Europe than in the US, but was unable to compete with IBM.
