{{Infobox software | name = Bump | title = | logo = [[File:Bumpapp.jpg]] | screenshot = | caption = The Bump logo as it appeared in [[Apple Inc.|Apple]]'s [[App Store]]. | author = Bump Technologies | released = | latest release version = 2.0.2 | latest release date = {{Start date|2010|08|10}} | frequently updated = yes | programming language = | operating system = [[iOS (Apple)|iOS]], [[Android (operating system)|Android]] | platform = | size = 2.2 [[megabyte|MB]] | language = [[English language|English]] | status = Active | genre = "Social Networking" | license = | website = [http://bu.mp/ www.bu.mp] }} '''Bump''' is an application created by Bump Technologies for the [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] [[iOS (Apple)|iOS]] and [[Google]] [[Android (operating system)|Android]] operating systems. It allows two [[smartphone]] users to bump their phones together to exchange contact information, photos, and other objects between phones. The billionth application downloaded on Apple's [[App Store]],{{cite web|title=Meet Bump, the App Store's Billionth Download|author=Dalrymple, Jim|date=April 24, 2009|publisher=[[PC World (magazine)|PC World]] |url=http://www.pcworld.com/article/163840/meet_bump_the_app_stores_billionth_download.html|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} it is free to download for both platforms.{{cite web|title=Review: Bump, the One Billionth iPhone App|author=Segan, Sascha|date=April 24, 2009|publisher=''[[PC Magazine]]'' |url=http://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2345899,00.asp|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} ==Functionality== Bump allows users to send contact information, photos, and other objects between phones. Before bumping phones, each user confirms what he or she wants to send to the other user. To use the application, two people bump their phones together, and within about five to ten seconds, a screen appears on both users' screens allowing them to confirm what they want to send to each other.{{cite web|title=New iPhone app works by bump, not touch|author=Benderoff, Eric|date=April 24, 2009|publisher=''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' |url=http://featuresblogs.chicagotribune.com/eric2_0/2009/03/new-iphone-app-works-by-bump-not-touch.html|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} When two users bump their phones, the data is automatically sent through a separate internet server to the other user, which is able to detect when any two phones using the application bump each other. A newer aspect of the application allows two people to bump in order to become [[Facebook]] friends.{{cite web|title=Facebook Friending Made Simple: Just Shake Your iPhone|author=Parr, Ben|publisher=[[Mashable]] |url=http://mashable.com/2010/02/25/bump-facebook/|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} Bump Technologies worked with [[PayPal]] in March 2010 to create a PayPal iPhone application.{{cite web|title=Owe Someone Money? Just Bump Your Phones|author=Miller, Clair Cain|date=March 15, 2010|publisher=''[[The New York Times]]'' |url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/03/15/owe-someone-money-just-bump-your-phones/o|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} The application allows two users to bump money between their accounts using the application, and has found widespread adoption.. A similar version was released for Android in August 2010.{{cite web|title=PayPal For Android Gets a “Bump”|author=Warren, Christina|publisher="[[Mashable]]"|date=August 6, 2010|url=http://mashable.com/2010/08/06/paypal-android/}} ==History== The idea for Bump was conceived by David Lieb, a former employee of [[Texas Instruments]], while he was attending the [[University of Chicago Booth School of Business]] for his [[MBA]]. While entering contact information into his [[iPhone]], he felt that the process could be improved. His fellow Texas Instrument employees Andy Huibers and Jake Mintz joined Lieb to form Bump Technologies. Funding for the project was provided by startup incubator [[Y Combinator]] and other [[angel investor]]s.{{cite web|title=Y Combinator Endorses Bump Technologies In The Quest To Destroy The Business Card|author=Kincaid, Jason|date=July 8, 2009|publisher=[[Techcrunch]] |url=http://techcrunch.com/2009/07/08/y-combinator-endorses-bump-technologies-in-the-quest-to-destroy-the-business-card/|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} It gained attention at the [[CTIA]] international wireless conference, due to its accessibility and novelty factor.{{cite web|title=Bump app draws buzz at CTIA|author=Hambien, Matt|date=October 8, 2009|publisher=''[[Computerworld]]'' |url=http://www.computerworld.com/s/article/9139163/Bump_app_draws_buzz_at_CTIA|accessdate=August 17, 2010}} ==References== {{reflist}} [[Category:IOS software]] [[Category:Android software]]
