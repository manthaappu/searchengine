{{see also | Foton}} '''Foton-M''' is series of [[robotic spacecraft]] used by [[Russia]] and the [[European Space Agency]] for research conducted in the [[microgravity]] environment of Earth [[orbit]]. The Foton-M design is based on the design of the [[Foton]], with several improvements including a new [[telemetry]] and [[telecommand]] unit for increased data flow rate, increased [[battery (electricity)|battery]] capacity, and a better thermal control system. It is produced by [[Progress State Research and Production Rocket Space Center|TsSKB-Progress]] in [[Samara, Russia|Samara]]. The first launch, of Foton-M1, failed because of a malfunction of the [[Soyuz-U]] [[launcher]]. The second launch, of Foton-M2, was however a success. {{cite web | url = http://www.esa.int/esaCP/SEMZDJ0DU8E_index_0.html | title = European experiments successfully launched aboard Foton spacecraft | publisher = ESA}} Foton-M3 was launched on 14 September 2007 carried by a [[Soyuz-U]] rocket lifting off from the [[Baikonur Cosmodrome]] in Kazakhstan. {{cite web | url = http://www.esa.int/esaCP/SEMQDB13J6F_index_0.html | title = Lift-off for Foton microgravity mission | publisher = ESA}} It returned successfully to Earth on September 26, 2007, landing in [[Kazakhstan]] at 7:58 [[GMT]]{{cite web |url=http://www.esa.int/esaCP/SEMFVO6H07F_index_0.html |title=Foton-M3 experiments return to Earth |accessdate= 2007-09-26 |work= }} == References == {{reflist}} {{Russia-spacecraft-stub}} [[es:Foton M]] [[Category:Robotics]] [[Category:Space science]]
