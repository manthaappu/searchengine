[[Image:Power-A5000.png|right|thumb|The Power A5000.]] The '''Power A5000''' was a planned [[Amiga]] clone computer, announced by [[DCE]] and [[Power Computing]]. It was one of the first Amiga clone computers announced after [[Gateway, Inc.|Gateway]] purchased the Amiga in 1997.{{citeweb |url=http://www.amigahistory.co.uk/power5000.html |title=The Power A5000. |publisher=Amiga history guide |date=2001-11-01 |accessdate=2008-11-15 }} {{citeweb |url=http://www.hehe.at/gamesworld/amigaindex.php?amiga=apowera5000 |title=Power A5000. |publisher=GamesworlD |date=2008-11-09 |accessdate=2008-11-15 }} The original [[Motorola 68000 family|Motorola 680x0]] based system was revised in favor of the [[PowerPC G3]] based system, since the future for the Amigas with 680x0 processors was very uncertain.
