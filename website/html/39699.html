'''W. Bruce Croft''' is a [[distinguished professor]] of [[computer science]] at the [[University of Massachusetts Amherst]] whose work focuses on [[information retrieval]]. {{Cite web | last = Croft | first = W. Bruce | title = Biography | url=http://ciir.cs.umass.edu/personnel/croftbio.pdf | accessdate = November 4, 2009}} He is the founder of the [[Center for Intelligent Information Retrieval]] and served as the editor-in-chief of [[ACM Transactions on Information Systems]] from 1995 to 2002. He was also a member of the [[United States National Research Council|National Research Council]] [http://sites.nationalacademies.org/CSTB/index.htm Computer Science and Telecommunications Board] from 2000 to 2003. ==Education== Croft earned a bachelor's degree with honors in 1973 and a master's degree in computer science in 1974 from [[Monash University]] in [[Melbourne|Melbourne, Australia]]. He earned his Ph.D in computer science from the [[University of Cambridge]] in 1979 and joined the UMass faculty later that year. ==Honors and awards== Croft was elected an [[ACM Fellow]] in 1997.
