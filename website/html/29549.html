{{Other uses|Polytomous}} {{Confusing|date=June 2010}} '''Polychotomous key''' refers the number of alternatives which a decision point may have in a non-temporal hierarchy of independent variables. The number of alternatives are equivalent to the root or [[nth root]] of a mathematical or logical variable. Decision points or independent variables with two states have a binary root that is referred to as a [[wiktionary:dichotomy|dichotomous]] key whereas the term polychotomous key refers to roots which are greater than one or unitary and usually greater than two or binary.
