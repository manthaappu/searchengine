'''PeakStream''' was a [[parallel processing]] software company located in San Mateo, California company founded by Matthew Papakipos and Asher Waldfogel in April 2005 and backed by [[Sequoia Capital]] and [[Kleiner Perkins]]. PeakStream released a high-performance parallel processing library targeting ATI graphics processors in September 2006.{{cite web|url=http://arstechnica.com/news.ars/post/20060918-7763.html|title=PeakStream unveils multicore and CPU/GPU programming solution}} It was acquired by [[Google]] on June 5, 2007.{{cite web|url=http://www.theregister.co.uk/2007/06/05/google_buys_peakstream/|title=Google shivs server crowd with PeakStream buy|publisher=[[The Register]]|date=2007-06-05|accessdate=2007-06-05}} ==References== {{Reflist}} ==External links== {{DEFAULTSORT:Peakstream}} [[Category:Parallel computing]] [[Category:Google acquisitions]] {{tech-company-stub}}
