{{Politics of France}} [[Image:Quai d'Orsay dsc06518.jpg|thumb|left|The honour entrance to the Ministry building on the Quai d'Orsay]] '''Ministry of Foreign Affairs''' ({{lang-fr|ministère des Affaires étrangères}}), is France's foreign affairs ministry, with the headquarters located on the [[Quai d'Orsay]] in [[Paris]] close to the [[National Assembly of France]]. The '''Minister of Foreign Affairs''' ({{lang-fr|ministre des Affaires étrangères}}) in the [[government of France]] is the [[French government ministers|cabinet minister]] responsible for the [[foreign relations of France]]. "Quai d'Orsay" is often used as a [[Metonymy|metonym]] for the ministry. Part of the ministry's central administration is located in [[Nantes]].{{Citation needed|date=September 2010}} The current minister is [[Michèle Alliot-Marie]], who also holds the title of Minister of European Affairs (''Ministre d’Etat, ministre des Affaires étrangères et européennes''). Secretaries to the King became specialized, writing correspondence to foreign governments, and negotiating peace treaties. The four French [[Secretary of state|secretaries of state]] where foreign relations were divided by region, in 1589, became centralized with one becoming first secretary responsible for [[international relations]]. The [[Ancien Régime in France|Ancien Régime]] position of [[Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs (France)|Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs]] became Foreign Minister around 1723,[http://books.google.com/books?id=ycUGAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA584&lpg=PA584&dq=Jacques+Bourdin,+seigneur+de+Villeines&source=bl&ots=OycrQe-cAu&sig=KzLWS1R0tQ9Y5hJRcwU0j_bVBno&hl=en&ei=1VBsS-v5IImaMqiasaEF&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=4&ved=0CBIQ6AEwAzgK#v=onepage&q=Jacques%20Bourdin%2C%20seigneur%20de%20Villeines&f=false ''Des origines du pouvoir ministériel en France: les secrétaires d'état depuis leur institution jusqu'à la mort de Louis XV'', Charles Hélion Marie le Gendre Luçay (comte de), Librairie de la Société bibliographique, 1881] and was renamed Minister of Foreign Affairs in 1791 after the [[French Revolution]]. All ministerial positions were abolished in 1794 by the [[National Convention]] and re-established with the [[French Directory|Directory]].
