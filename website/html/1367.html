{{for|the concept in modal logic|Subjunctive possibility}} '''Alethic modality''' is a [[linguistic modality]] which indicates logical necessity, possibility or impossibility.{{cite web|url=http://www.sil.org/linguistics/GlossaryOflinguisticTerms/WhatIsAlethicModality.htm|title=What is alethic modality?|publisher=SIL International|work=Glossary of linguistic terms|last=Loos|first=Eugene E.|coauthors=Susan Anderson; Dwight H. Day, Jr.; Paul C. Jordan; J. Douglas Wingate|accessdate=2010-01-03}} It has been questioned whether this modality should be considered distinct from [[epistemic modality]] which denotes the speaker's evaluation or judgment of the truth.
