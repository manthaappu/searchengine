'''Complex systems biology''' ('''CSB''') is a branch or subfield of [[mathematical and theoretical biology]] concerned with [[complexity]] of both structure and function in biological organisms, as well as the emergence and evolution of organisms and species, with emphasis being placed on the [[interconnectivity|complex interactions]] of, and within, [[Biological network inference|bionetworks]] {{cite book |author= Sprites, P; Glymour, C; Scheines, R |year=2000 |title= Causation, Prediction, and Search: Adaptive Computation and Machine Learning |edition=2nd |publisher=[[MIT Press]] |isbn= }}, and on the [[Relations|fundamental relations]] and [[Relational algebra|relational patterns]] that are essential to lifeGraeme Donald Snooks, "A general theory of complex living systems: Exploring the demand side of dynamics", Complexity, vol. 13, no. 6, July/August 2008.Bonner, J. T. 1988. The Evolution of Complexity by Means of Natural Selection. Princeton: Princeton University Press.Rosen, R.: 1958a, "A Relational Theory of Biological Systems". Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics 20: 245-260Baianu, I. C.: 2006, "Robert Rosen's Work and Complex Systems Biology", Axiomathes 16(1-2):25-34Rosen, R.: 1958b, "The Representation of Biological Systems from the Standpoint of the Theory of Categories.", Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics 20: 317-341.. '''CSB''' is thus a field of theoretical sciences aimed at discovering and [[Relational model|modeling the relational patterns]] essential to life that has only a partial overlap with [[complex systems theory]]http://www.springerlink.com/index/V1RT05876H74V607.pdf I. C. Baianu, R. Brown and J. F. Glazebrook. 2007. Categorical Ontology of Complex Spacetime Structures: The Emergence of Life and Human Consciousness., ''Axiomathes'', 17:223–352. doi: 10.1007/s10516-007-9011-2 . , and also with the systems approach to biology called [[systems biology]]; this is because the latter is restricted primarily to simplified models of biological organization and organisms, as well as to only a general consideration of philosophical or semantic questions related to complexity in biologyhttp://www.semantic-systems-biology.org/ Semantic Systems Biology Portal. Moreover, a wide range of abstract theoretical [[complex systems]] are studied as a field of [[applied mathematics]], with or without relevance to biology, chemistry or physics.
