'''Social media and suicide''' have important relationships. The [[death of Phoebe Prince]] was said to have resulted from [[cyberbullying]]. There is some concern that the desire for achieving prestige via [[online memorial]]s may encourage suicides.{{citation|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/1576338/Police-fear-internet-cult-inspires-teen-suicides.html|title=Police fear internet cult inspires teen suicides|author=Nick Britten and Richard Savill|date=23 Jan 2008|publisher=Telegraph}} [[Vincent Nichols]], the [[Archbishop of Westminster]], has warned that popular social networking sites lead young people to form "transient relationships" which put them at risk of suicide when they collapse.{{citation|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/idUSTRE5710CR20090802?feedType=RSS&feedName=internetNews|title=Head of English Catholics warns about emails/texting|publisher=Reuters | first=Michael | last=Holden | date=August 2, 2009}} ==Suicide notes== It has generally been found that those who post suicide notes online tend to not receive help.{{citation|url=http://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/promoting-hope-preventing-suicide/200904/can-social-media-help-prevent-suicide|title=Can Social Media Help Prevent Suicide?|publisher=Psychology Today|date=April 6, 2009|author=Elana Premack Sandler}} [[Paul Zolezzi]]{{citation|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/2009/02/20/2009-02-20_facebook_status_update_becomes_suicide_n.html|title=Facebook status update becomes suicide note for aspiring Brooklyn model, actor Paul Zolezzi|author=Gendar, Alison and Connor, Tracy|publisher=New York Daily News}} indicated via a [[Facebook]] update his intent to commit suicide. In 2010, [[John Patrick Bedell]] left a [[Wikipedia]] user page and [[YouTube]] videos interpreted by some as a suicide note; the former was deleted by Wikipedia administrators.{{citation|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-504083_162-6269989-504083.html|title=John Patrick Bedell: Rants on Wikipedia and YouTube May Have Foreshadowed Breakdown|publisher=CBS News|author=Carlin DeGuerin Miller|date=March 5, 2010}} [[Joe Stack]] also posted a suicide note online.{{citation|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-504083_162-6220533-504083.html|title=Joe Stack Suicide Note Full Text: "American Zombies Wake Up and Revolt"|author=Neil Katz|publisher=CBS News|date=February 18, 2010}} ==Webcasted suicides== [[Kevin Whitrick]] and [[Abraham K. Biggs]] [[webcasted]] their suicides. ==Suicide pacts== [[Gerald Krein]]{{citation|url=http://www.cnn.com/2005/US/02/12/valentine.suicide/index.html|title=Sheriff: Online suicide pact had sexual overtones|publisher=CNN | date=February 13, 2005}} and [[William Francis Melchert-Dinkel]] were accused of arranging [[internet suicide pact]]s. ==Interventions== [[Demi Moore]] and her followers intervened to stop a suicide that had been announced on [[Twitter]].{{citation|url=http://news.cnet.com/8301-17852_3-10212297-71.html?tag=mncol;txt|author=Chris Matyszczyk|date=April 3, 2009|publisher=CNet}} ==Discussion and support groups== Some online groups, such as [[alt.suicide.holiday]], have emerged as discussion and support groups for suicidal individuals. Research indicates that providing more online support for suicidal people would be more effective than shutting down [[pro-suicide]] websites.{{citation|url=http://www.sciencealert.com.au/news/20090308-19499.html|title=Online suicide support needed|date=03 August 2009|publisher=[[Science Alert]]}} The [[Defense Centers of Excellence]] have expressed interest in using social media for [[suicide prevention]].{{citation|url=http://www.dcoe.health.mil/blog/article.aspx?id=1&postid=63|title=Harnessing New and Social Media to Prevent Suicide|publisher=Defense Centers of Excellence|date=January 22, 2010}} [[Facebook group]]s have sometimes been set up for suicide prevention purposes,{{citation|url=http://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/promoting-hope-preventing-suicide/201006/suicide-prevention-in-cyberspace|title=Suicide prevention in cyberspace|date=June 16, 2010|author=Elana Premack Sandler|publisher=Psychology Today}} including one that attracted 47,000 members.{{citation|url=http://www.vancouversun.com/life/Anti+suicide+Facebook+group+elicits+positive+messages/3151586/story.html|title=Anti-suicide Facebook group elicits positive messages|author=Minsky, Amy|publisher=The Vancouver Sun}} [[SAMHSA]]'s [[Suicide Prevention Lifeline]] operates on Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube.{{citation|url=http://www.samhsa.gov/samhsaNewsletter/Volume_18_Number_2/SuicidePreventionLifeline.aspx|title=Suicide Prevention Lifeline Update|author=SAMHSA}} ==References== {{reflist}} ==See also== *[[Social media and death]] *[[Suicide and the Internet]] [[Category:Suicide]] [[Category:Internet culture]] [[Category:Social network services]] [[Category:Social media]]
