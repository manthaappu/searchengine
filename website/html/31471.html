She is affiliated with the [[Georgia Institute of Technology Center for Robotics and Intelligent Machines|RIM@GT]] and the [[GVU Center]]. Grinter's research lies generally in the fields of [[human-computer interaction]] (HCI) and [[computer-supported cooperative work]] (CSCW). She has chaired and published papers in top-tier academic conferences in these fields. Her research and expert opinion on technology have also been reported in major news media sources. ==Biography== ===Early life and education=== Grinter was born in [[Norwich]], [[England]], [[UK]] and attended the [[City of Norwich School]] and [[Hethersett High School]].{{ cite web | url=http://www.grinter.org/Beki.html | last=Grinter | first=Beki | title=Beki: Academic | accessdate=2008-03-28 }} She pursued undergraduate studies at the [[University of Leeds]], graduating in 1991 with a [[Bachelor of Science|B.Sc.]] (Hons) in [[Computer Science]]. Following this, Grinter received a [[Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council|EPSRC]] fellowship to study at the [[University of California, Irvine]], where she earned a [[Master of Science|M.S.]] (1994) and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] (1996), both in Information and Computer Science. As a graduate student, Grinter was influenced by [[David Parnas]], whose work in software engineering spoke to Grinter's burgeoning interest in the relationship between humans and software.{{ cite book | title=HCI Remixed: Essays on Works That Have Influenced the HCI Community | last=Grinter | first=Rebecca E. | chapter=The Work to Make Software Work | year=2008 | publisher=MIT Press | location=Cambridge, MA, USA | pages=97–101 | editor=Thomas Erickson and David W. McDonald (Eds.) }} Grinter's dissertation, titled, "Understanding Dependencies: A Study of the Coordination Challenges in Software Development", was chaired by [[Jonathan Grudin]].{{ cite web | last=Grinter | first=Rebecca E. | url=http://portal.acm.org/citation.cfm?coll=GUIDE&dl=GUIDE&id=923458 | accessdate=2008-03-26 | title=Understanding dependencies: a study of the coordination challenges in software development | publisher=[[University of California, Irvine]] | year=1996 }} ===Research career=== Upon receiving her Ph.D. from the University of California, Irvine, Grinter worked at [[Bell Labs]] in [[Naperville, Illinois]] as a Technical Staff Member from 1996 to 2000. She then left Bell Labs to accept a position as a Research Staff Member at [[Palo Alto Research Center]] (PARC) from 2000 to 2004.
