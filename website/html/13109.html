[[Image:FoafLogo.svg|thumb|FOAF logo.]] '''FOAF''' (an acronym of '''[[Friend of a friend]]''') is a [[machine-readable]] [[Ontology (computer science)|ontology]] describing [[person]]s, their activities and their relations to other people and objects. Anyone can use FOAF to describe him or herself. FOAF allows groups of people to describe [[social networks]] without the need for a centralised database. FOAF is a descriptive vocabulary expressed using the [[Resource Description Framework]] (RDF) and the [[Web Ontology Language]] (OWL). Computers may use these FOAF profiles to find, for example, all people living in Europe, or to list all people both you and a friend of yours know[http://www.ibm.com/developerworks/xml/library/x-foaf.html XML Watch: Finding friends with XML and RDF] by Edd Dumbill in [[IBM DeveloperWorks]][http://www.ibm.com/developerworks/xml/library/x-foaf2.html XML Watch: Support online communities with FOAF] by Edd Dumbill in [[IBM DeveloperWorks]]. This is accomplished by defining relationships between people. Each profile has a unique identifier (such as the person's [[e-mail address]]es, a [[Extensible Messaging and Presence Protocol|Jabber ID]], or a [[Uniform Resource Identifier|URI]] of the homepage or weblog of the person), which is used when defining these relationships. The FOAF project, which defines and extends the vocabulary of a FOAF profile, was started in 2000 by [[Libby Miller]] and [[Dan Brickley (computer scientist)|Dan Brickley]]. It can be considered the first [[Social Semantic Web]] application, in that it combines RDF technology with 'Social Web' concerns. [[Tim Berners-Lee]] in a 2007 essay [http://dig.csail.mit.edu/breadcrumbs/node/215 Giant Global Graph], Tim Berners Lee redefined the [[Semantic web]] concept into something he calls the [[Giant Global Graph]], where relationships transcend networks/documents. He considers the GGG to be on equal grounds with [[Internet]] and [[World Wide Web]], stating that "I express my network in a FOAF file, and that is a start of the revolution." ==FOAF+SSL== FOAF+SSL[http://esw.w3.org/topic/foaf+ssl FOAF+SSL] decentralized authentication protocol. is the decentralized secure authentication protocol utilizing the FOAF profile information as well as the [[Transport Layer Security|SSL security layer]] usually available in virtually all modern web browsers. It was first presented[http://blogs.sun.com/bblfish/entry/foaf_ssl_adding_security_to foaf+ssl: adding security to open distributed social networks] - for the "[http://www.w3.org/2008/09/msnws/ W3C Workshop on the Future of Social Networking]", which took place in Barcelona January 2009 for the W3C Workshop on the Future of Social Networking in 2009. Contrary to the usual SSL utilization patterns, it does not requirehttp://blogs.sun.com/bblfish/entry/more_on_authorization_in_foaf - foaf+ssl: creating a web of trust without key signing parties the dedicated key signing parties such as [[Certificate authority]] to perform the user authorization. ==Deployment== Being an early example of a Semantic Web application, and having a relatively simple use-case and standard has led FOAF to be adopted widely on the web.
