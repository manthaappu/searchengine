{{Unreferenced|date=December 2009}} If L_1 and L_2 are [[formal languages]], then the '''left quotient''' of L_1 with L_2 is the language consisting of strings ''w'' such that ''xw'' is in L_2 for some string ''x'' in L_1. In symbols, we write: L_1 \backslash L_2 = \{w \ | \ \exists x ((x \in L_1) \land (xw \in L_2))\} You can regard the '''left quotient''' as the set of postfixes that complete words from L_1, such that the resulting word is in L_2. For more details, see [[right quotient]]. {{DEFAULTSORT:Left Quotient}} [[Category:Formal languages]]
