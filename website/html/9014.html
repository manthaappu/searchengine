{{Orphan|date=January 2011}} '''DJ Tutor''' is the title given to Johnathan Lewis, and to the online community of [[DJ]]s that he inspired through his [[Youtube]] channel "ellaskins". == Background == Johnathan's rough and ready, punk TV style was a contrast to the slick image that DJs were presenting previously. His tutorial videos for beginners have started the careers of many professional DJs."[http://www.myspace.com/djpetermorgan DJ Peter Morgan]""[http://www.myspace.com/djbrettland DJ Brettland]""[http://syntse.harder.fi/bio.html Harder Fi]""[http://www.youtube.com/profile?user=bamanda2 DJ Ama]""[http://www.youtube.com/djarkitech DJ Arkitech]" Thousands of new and experienced DJs have responded with videos and comments, so that DJ Tutor has become a social network for DJs from all over the world. Johnathan's catchphrase "Practise and N_Joy" has entered the DJ vocabulary."[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-3-Nl1Vmaw Grandmaster Flash interview]""[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5NRjoNZ_caE Sugar Hill Gang, Coolio and DJ Kool interviews]""[http://www.ytdjs.com/ Youtube DJs]""[http://funktiondjs.com/Funktion-blog(1595427).htm Funktion blog]""[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6tpNA1xcN8w Dj Fi:T]" == Johnathan Lewis== Johnathan grew up in [[Borth]], near [[Aberystwyth]], and still lives and works in [[West Wales]]. He started working as a professional [[DJ]] in 1984. Clubs and hotels he worked in include Starlight ([[New York]]), Tiffany's ([[Oslo]]) and the Continental Hotel ([[Isle of Man]]). He also presented his own show "Phatbeats FM" on [[Radio Ceredigion]], as well as running a mobile roadshow. == History == In September 2006 Johnathan discovered Youtube, and started posting comedy videos, and drum tutorials. In October 2006 Johnathan noticed the lack of DJ tutorials, and decided to fill the gap. Encouraged by the response, he singlehandedly produced several DJ videos a day. In 2007, the DJ Tutor website was launched, and DJ Tutor became a limited company. DJs were asked to post their own ten minute mix videos, which they did in thousands, and this led to the first ever online video DJ mix competition. DJ Tutor ran the [[DJ Mag]] Micromix competition,"[http://www.olddjmag.com/?op=story&story_op=display&story_id=723&status=Live&word=&page= DJ Mag Micromix Competition]" plus several other competitions. DJ equipment manufacturers started to send products for use in the videos, and embedded the videos and links in their own websites."[http://www.djsounds.com/taxonomy/term/721 Pioneer - DJ Sounds]""[http://www.kam.co.uk/index.php?action=product&product_id=340 Kam]" The Youtube channel "ellaskins" was included in the book Youtube For Dummies.Doug Sahlin, Chris Botello, ''Wiley Publishing'' ISBN 978-0470149256 Johnathan has contributed articles to [[DJ Mag]], [[Mixmag]] and Pro Mobile. Other DJs began to contribute, notably Brian Redd, John Beck and DJ Angelo. Johnathan was invited to the BPM show, [[Musikmesse]], and Mobile Beat Las Vegas, and became a Tutor at [[Ministry of Sound]] DJ Academy."[http://djacademy.ministryofsound.com/djs.asp Ministry of Sound DJ Academy]" He has taught DJing for [[BBC Blast]] and DJ Tutor is on the [[BBC]]'s list of music learning resources."[http://www.bbc.co.uk/learning/subjects/music.shtml BBC learning resources,]" On 2 October 2010, DJ Tutor and BPM Show attempted to break the [[Guinness Book of Records]] world record for longest DJ mix.
