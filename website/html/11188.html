Insertion Loss typical < 1.0 dB]] A '''duplexer''' is a device that allows bi-directional ([[duplex (telecommunications)|duplex]]) communication over a single channel. In [[radar]] and radio communications systems, it isolates the [[receiver (radio)|receiver]] from the [[transmitter]] while permitting them to share a common [[antenna (electronics)|antenna]]. Most [[radio repeater]] systems include a duplexer. ''Note 1:'' A duplexer must be designed for operation in the [[frequency]] [[band (radio)|band]] used by the receiver and transmitter, and must be capable of handling the [[output]] [[Power (physics)|power]] of the transmitter. ''Note 2:'' A duplexer must provide adequate rejection of transmitter [[noise]] occurring at the receive frequency, and must be designed to operate at, or less than, the frequency separation between the transmitter and receiver. ''Note 3:'' A duplexer must provide sufficient isolation to prevent receiver desensitization.
