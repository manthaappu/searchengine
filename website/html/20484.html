{{for|the football player of the same name|Keith Bostic (American football)}} {{infobox person | name = Keith Bostic | birth_date = {{birth date and age|1959|07|26}} | employer = [[UC Berkeley]] [[Computer Systems Research Group|CSRG]]; later, (until Feb. 2008), {{nowrap begin}}[[Sleepycat Software]]{{nowrap end}} / {{nowrap begin}}[[Oracle Corporation]]{{nowrap end}} | known = [[nvi]], [[Free and open source software|FLOSS]] version of [[BSD UNIX]] | website = [http://www.bostic.com/keith.html www.bostic.com/keith.html] }} '''Keith Bostic''' (born July 26, 1959) is a [[computer programmer]] from the [[United States]]. In 1986, Bostic joined the [[Computer Systems Research Group]] (CSRG) at the [[University of California, Berkeley]]. He was one of the principal architects of the Berkeley 4.4BSD and 4.4BSD-Lite releases.{{cite web |author=Dale Dougherty |date=2000-03-24 |title=Bostic on the BSD Tradition: An interview with BSD veteran Keith Bostic |url=http://www.onlamp.com/pub/a/bsd/2000/03/24/bostic.html |publisher=O'Reilly Media, Inc. |work=ONLamp.com: BSD DevCenter |accessdate=2008-04-05}} Among many other tasks, he led the effort at CSRG to create a [[free software]] version of [[BSD UNIX]], which helped allow the creation of [[FreeBSD]], [[NetBSD]] and [[OpenBSD]]. Bostic was also one of the founders of [[Berkeley Software Design Inc.]] (BSDi), which produced [[BSD/OS]], a proprietary version of BSD. He and wife [[Margo Seltzer]] founded [[Sleepycat Software]] in 1996 to develop and support [[Berkeley DB]], an open source database. In February 2006, the company was acquired by [[Oracle Corporation]], where Bostic worked until February 2008.
