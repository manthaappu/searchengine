{{Otheruses}} {{Unreferenced|date=November 2009}} In [[algebra]], a '''binomial''' is a [[polynomial]] with two terms—the sum of two [[monomial]]s—often bound by parenthesis or brackets when operated upon. It is the simplest kind of polynomial. For example, (x + 5) is a binomial of [[degree of a polynomial|degree]] one, while (x^2 + 5) is ''still'' a binomial, but it is a binomial of ''degree two''. ==See also== *[[Binomial theorem]] *[[Completing the square]] *[[Binomial distribution]] *[[Binomial coefficient]] * [[Binomial-QMF]] (Daubechies Wavelet Filters) *The [[list of factorial and binomial topics]] contains a large number of related links. *[[Binomial series]] ==References== * L.
