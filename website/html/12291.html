In [[quantum mechanics]], and especially [[quantum information processing]], the '''entropy exchange''' of a [[quantum operation]] \phi \, acting on the [[density matrix]] \rho_Q \, of a system Q \, is defined as :S(\rho,\phi) \equiv S[Q',R'] = S(\rho_{QR}') where S(\rho_{QR}') \, is the [[von Neumann entropy]] of the system Q \, and a fictitious [[Purification of quantum state|purifying]] auxiliary system R \, after they are operated on by \phi \,. Here, :\rho_{QR} = |QR\rangle\langle QR| \quad , :\mathrm{Tr}_R[\rho_{QR}] = \rho_Q \quad , and :\rho_{QR}' = \phi[\rho_{QR}] \quad . ==References== * Nielsen, Michael A. and [[Isaac L. Chuang]] (2000). ''Quantum Computation and Quantum Information'', Cambridge University Press, ISBN 0-521-63503-9 [[Category:Quantum information science]] {{Quantum-stub}}
