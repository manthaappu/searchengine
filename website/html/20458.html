{{Like resume|date=March 2009}} {{POV|date=August 2010}} '''Kate Craig-Wood''' (born 1977) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] IT [[entrepreneur]] and the co-founder and managing director of Memset Dedicated Hosting. She is also a champion for the causes of energy efficiency, especially in the data centre, and women in IT.{{cite news|url=http://news.zdnet.co.uk/itmanagement/0,1000000308,39290823,00.htm|title=MP: Tech needs more female role models|last=Meyer|first=David|work=ZDNet UK|date=15 November 2007}} ==Career== Craig-Wood has always had an interest in science, technology and business. She taught herself a smattering of programming languages and internet technologies, and after completing her MSc is in Biomedical science joined [[Arthur Andersen]] business consulting as an IT consultant. She went on to head business development for Easyspace Ltd., one of the UK's largest [[web hosting]] companies, and subsequently formed Memset with her brother, Nick, in late 2002. Memset has grown rapidly since inception, and Craig-Wood's foci on technology, innovation and corporate social responsibility have helped to secure Memset's position as a leading dedicated Web & IT hosting provider. Memset is Britain's first [[carbon neutral]] ISP,{{cite web|url=http://www.enterprisehubnetwork.co.uk/magazine-article.php?id=71|title=Memset goes Carbon Neutral|work=Hub Magazine|publisher=Enterprise Hub Network}} has been voted best UK Web host two years running (2006 & 2007){{cite news|url=http://www.pcpro.co.uk/news/143886/|title=PCPro award winners 2007|work=PC Pro|date=4 December 2007}} and has recently won a number of other awards for innovation, environmental awareness and IT strategy.[http://www.memset.com/news.php MEMSET Hosting : News] Craig-Wood also takes an active interest in local and national business affairs. Her other positions include director of the Surrey Chambers of Commerce, a committee member of the Institute of Directors [[West Surrey]] branch, and is a director of [[Intellect UK]], the UK's high-tech trade association.{{cite news|url=http://www.womenintechnology.co.uk/news/female-entrepreneur-joins-intellect-uk-board-news-18160735|title=Female entrepreneur joins Intellect's board|work=Women in Technology|date=25 May 2007}} Craig-Wood has a passion for environmental issues and is a champion of the cause of energy-efficient computing, speaking frequently on the subject. She is part of Intellect UK's directorial leadership group on energy and efficiency, and is very involved of the [[British Computer Society|British Computer Society's]] efforts on green IT via her committee membership of the [http://dcsg.bcs.org/ Data Centre Specialist Group]. ==Transsexual activism== Craig-Wood, born male and originally named Robert, completed a successful transsexual transition between October 2005 and November 2006. She wants to help transsexual people in the UK. In March 2008 she "came out" in the Sunday Times magazine in the hope that she might be the role model to younger transwomen that she never had, and also to try and dispel some myths about the transgendered.{{Cite news|url=http://women.timesonline.co.uk/tol/life_and_style/women/the_way_we_live/article3620849.ece|title=Best of Times, Worst of Times: Kate Craig-Wood|newspaper=The Sunday Times|date=30 March 2008}} She is also an executive committee member and trustee of the Gender Identity Research and Education Society, and is working with the group with a focus on improving medical care in the UK for young transpeople. == References == {{Reflist}} ==External links== *[http://kate.craig-wood.com/ Kate's personal website]. *[http://www.memset.com/ Memset Dedicated Hosting]. *[http://www.gires.org.uk/ The Gender Identity Research & Education Society]. {{Persondata | NAME = Craig-Wood, Kate | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = | DATE OF BIRTH = 1977 | PLACE OF BIRTH = | DATE OF DEATH = | PLACE OF DEATH = }} {{DEFAULTSORT:Craig-Wood, Kate}} [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:1977 births]] [[Category:Transgender and transsexual people]] [[Category:Women computer scientists]] [[Category:English chief executives]] [[Category:LGBT people from England]]
