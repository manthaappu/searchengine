[[Manfred Glesner]] }} '''Hartmut Surmann''' (born [[1963]] in [[D端lmen]], [[Germany]]) is a [[Roboticist]], Professor for Autonomous Systems at Applied University of Gelsenkirchen and Researcher at the [[Fraunhofer]] Institut Intelligente Analyse- und Informationssystem (IAIS). His primary research interests are autonomous mobile robotics and computational intelligence. He received several awards, e.g., the FUZZ-IEEE/IFES'95 robot intelligence award, NC2001 best presentation award, SSRR 2005 best paper award and the Ph.D. award for his thesis from the German AI institutes in 1996. His robot KURT3D won the second place in the [[RoboCup]] rescue robot league at the world championship in Lisbon in 2004.
