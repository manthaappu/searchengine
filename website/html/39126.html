{{orphan|date=October 2009}} '''VISQ''' (Portuguese acronym for "Variables that Interact Semi-Quantitatively") is a scientific-educational [[software]] developed in 1993 by M. Thielo (as an [[physics]] undergrad at the time) and Kurtz dos Santos, based on Jon Ogborn ideas for semi-quantitative modeling of [[dynamical systems]], for both [[MS-DOS]] and [[Macintosh]] systems. Awarded by the [[Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul]] (UFRGS) in 1994, the system is based on the theory of continuous [[neural networks]], and have been used in many research projects since then, mainly in Master Dissertations in the area of [[environmental education]]. An introduction to VISQ (in Portuguese) can be found at the university site [http://www.modelciencias.furg.br/bin/ferramentas/semi/visq.php VISQ: and introduction to semiquantitative modelling tools(in portuguese)], and the [[software]] can be freely downloaded from the author's site[http://www.if.ufrgs.br/~thielo/visq.zip Downloading VISQ for MS-DOS] ==References== ==External links== *[http://www.modelciencias.furg.br/bin/ferramentas/semi/visq.php] Representing Semiquantitative Variables with VISQ (in Portuguese) *[http://www3.interscience.wiley.com/journal/119171348/abstract] Journal Article containing a case study with the program, published in the International Journal of Computer Assisted Learning *[http://www.fisica.furg.br/profecomp/artigos/MSET99.pdf] Article published in the annals of the Mathematics/Science Education and Technology conference(M/SET 99) *[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DwJq_U22d74] Animated example of a semiquantitative model running under VISQ.
