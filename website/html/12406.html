'''Eric A. Brewer''' is the main [[inventor]] of a [[wireless networking]] scheme called [[WiLDNet]] which promises to bring low-cost connectivity to rural areas of the [[developing world]]. He also was made a [[tenure]]d [[professor]] at [[University of California, Berkeley|UC Berkeley]] at the age of 32. In 1996, Brewer co-founded [[Inktomi Corporation]]. He is known for formulating the [[CAP Theorem]] about distributed network applications.{{cite web|title=Lessons from Internet Services: ACID vs. BASE|url=http://www.ccs.neu.edu/groups/IEEE/ind-acad/brewer/sld009.htm|accessdate=2008-11-06 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080624003730/http://www.ccs.neu.edu/groups/IEEE/ind-acad/brewer/sld009.htm |archivedate = 2008-06-24}} ==Education== Brewer received a [[Bachelor of Science|BS]] in [[EECS|Electrical Engineering and Computer Science]] (EECS) from [[University of California, Berkeley|UC Berkeley]] and later an [[Master of Science|MS]] and [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in EECS from [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT]]. ==Awards== Eric Brewer is the [http://www.acm.org/news/featured/acm-infosys-09 2009 recipient] of the ACM-Infosys Foundation Award in the Computing Sciences {{cite web | url = http://awards.acm.org/homepage.cfm?srt=abc&awd=165 | title = List of recipients of the ACM-InfoSys Foundation Award | publisher = ACM (the [[Association for Computing Machinery]]) | archiveurl = | archivedate = | accessdate = 25 June 2010 }} ''"for his contributions to the design and development of highly scalable Internet services."''
