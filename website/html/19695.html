'''Jan Vanthienen''' (1954) is a Belgium [[organizational theorist]] and Professor of [[Information systems]] at [[Catholic University of Leuven|Leuven University]] (K.U.Leuven), known for his contributions to [[Business Process Modeling]] and [[Business Engineering]].[http://www.informatik.uni-trier.de/~ley/db/indices/a-tree/v/Vanthienen:Jan.html Jan Vanthienen] List of publications from the DBLP Bibliography Server. Accessed June 21, 2009. == Biography == Vanthienen received MA in Applied Economics and computer science at the [[Leuven University]] in 1979, and a PhD in Applied Economics at the Department of Applied Economics, [[Leuven University]], in 1986.[http://www.econ.kuleuven.be/eng/fetew/medewerker/cv.aspx?PID=271 Curriculum Vitae - Jan Vanthienen]. Accessed June 21, 2009. He started working as a Researcher at the [[Leuven University]] in 1979, where he became an Associate professor in 1987, a Professor in 1994, and a Full Professor in 1996. At the moment, in 2009, he is chairholder of the PricewaterhouseCoopers Chair on E-Business at K.U.Leuven and co-chairholder of the Microsoft Research Chair on Intelligent Environments. He has been a visiting professor at Loyola College in Maryland, Sellinger School of Business and Management, [[Baltimore, Md]] in 1991, at the [[University of Antwerp]] since 1998 and at the Vlerick Leuven Gent Management School from 2000 to 2003. Vanthienen has been a member of the Editorial Board of the journal "Informatie" from 1995 to 1999, "International Journal of Digital Accounting" since 2001 and "Expert Systems with Applications" since 2002. He is a founding member of the Leuven Institute for Research in Information Systems (LIRIS), and a member of the [[Association for Computing Machinery|ACM]] and the [[IEEE Computer Society]]. == Work == His research interests are in the field of [[Information systems]]: Information systems analysis and design, business rules & business processes, business intelligence, knowledge-based systems, intelligent systems, object-oriented development, decision tables, [[e-learning]], [[information management]]. More specific research topics are Business rule modeling, rule-based business processes, [[E-business]], [[business intelligence]], [[Artificial Intelligence Marketing]], [[knowledge based system]]s, V&V, decision tables, help desk and service automation.[http://www.econ.kuleuven.ac.be/tew/academic/infosys/members/vthienen/default.htm Jan Vanthienen] Personal homepage.
