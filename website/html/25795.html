{{otheruses4|the American actor|the English actor sometimes known as Nat Parker|Nathaniel Parker}} {{Infobox actor | image = 20080906 Nate Parker.jpg | imagesize = 250px | caption = Parker at the [[2008 Toronto International Film Festival|2008 Toronto Film Festival]] on September 6 | birthdate = {{birth date and age|1979|11|18}} | birthplace = [[Norfolk, Virginia]] | birthname = Nate Parker | othername = | occupation = Actor | yearsactive = 2004-present | spouse = Sarah DiSanto (2007-) }} '''Nate Parker''' (born November 18, 1979) is an American actor and musical performer who has appeared in ''[[The Secret Life of Bees (film)|The Secret Life of Bees]]'', ''[[The Great Debaters]]'', and ''[[Pride (2007 film)|Pride]]''. In his recent roles, he has performed alongside [[Denzel Washington]], [[Forest Whitaker]] and [[Terrence Howard]]. Parker has overcome turbulence and turmoil in his life both as a youth and a collegian. He was raised in both [[Virginia]] and [[Maine]], but blossomed as a wrestler in his later high school years in Virginia. Parker was an [[All-American]] [[wrestler]] in both high school and at the [[University of Oklahoma]].{{cite web|url=http://louisianamovies.blogspot.com/2007/12/great-debaters-interviews-nate-parker.html|title='The Great Debaters' interviews: Nate Parker Plays Brilliant, Volatile Mind|accessdate=2008-11-07|date=2007-12-24|work=[[Shreveport Times]]|author=Kent, Alexandyr}} Parker has been active in charitable work, donating his time both as a volunteer wrestling coach and a political activist. ==Background== Parker was born in [[Norfolk, Virginia]],{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1676649/|title=Nate Parker|accessdate=2008-11-07|publisher=IMDb.com, Inc.|work=[[IMDb.com]]}} to Carolyn, a 17 year-old mother at the time. His mother never married his biological father, but did wed the man who gave Parker his last name. Parker has four younger sisters.{{cite web|url=http://www.movietome.com/people/284622/nate-parker/trivia.html|title=Nate Parker|accessdate=2008-11-07|publisher=CNET Networks, Inc.|worl=MovieTome}} During Parker's time in middle school, Carolyn fell in love with Walter Whitfield, who was stationed in [[Bath, Maine]] in the [[United States Air Force]]. Although his mother married Whitfield and moved to Maine, Parker, despite the physical distance after the move, remained close to his biological father emotionally. His father died when Parker was eleven, however. Parker became involved in classroom fights and home power struggles with his stepfather, Whitfield. Parker moved to [[Virginia Beach, Virginia]] to stay with his maternal uncle Jay Combs, a former wrestler, at age 14. Nate's great-grandmother got sick and his mother moved back to [[Hampton Roads, Virginia]] and lived in the [[Churchland High School]] zone, where Nate joined her.{{cite web|url=http://hamptonroads.com/2007/12/hampton-roads-hollywood|title=From Hampton Roads to Hollywood|accessdate=2008-11-08|date=2007-12-23|publisher=HamptonRoads.com|work=[[The Virginian-Pilot]] online|author=Tucker, Kyle}} As a sophomore at [[Pennsylvania State University]] in 1999, he and a teammate were accused of having had [[non-consensual sex]] with a woman who had passed out, but when she admitted during the trial to having had [[consensual sex]] with Parker the day before the alleged rape, Parker was acquitted in 2001. Parker married [[Erie, Pennsylvania]]-native Sarah DiSanto, whom he met while they were attending Penn State, in Erie's Frontier Park during the summer of 2007.{{cite web|url=http://www.goerie.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?Date=20071124&Category=NEWS02&ArtNo=711240381&SectionCat=SHOWCASE&Template=printart|title=Actor with Erie ties to appear on 'Oprah'|accessdate=2008-11-08|date=2007-11-24|publisher=goerie.com}}{{cite web|url=http://registry.weddingchannel.com/coupledir/20077/D/O567511676/SARAH_DISANTO_AND_NATE_PARKER.htm|title=SARAH DISANTO and NATE PARKER Wedding|accessdate=2008-11-08|publisher=WeddingChannel.com|work=Wedding Couples Directory}} {{Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot}} He is a University of Oklahoma alumnus.{{cite web|url=http://www.essence.com/news_entertainment/entertainment/articles/nateparkernewcomerhitsitbig|title=Nate Parker: Newcomer Hits it Big|accessdate=2008-11-07|date=2007|publisher=Essence Communications Inc.|work=[[Essence (magazine)|Essence]]|author=Robertson, Regina R.}} ==Athletic career== Because Parker looked up to Combs, Combs was able to get Parker started in Wrestling at [[Princess Anne High School]]. Parker placed third in the [[Virginia High School League]] state wrestling championships as a junior while attending Churchland after reuniting with his mother. Carolyn Whitfield moved to the [[Great Bridge High School]] district so Parker could participate in its powerhouse wrestling program. He was a member of the 1997–98 state champion Great Bridge wrestling team and was a state champion {{convert|135|lb|kg|adj=on}} wrestler who placed third in the High School National Wrestling Championships, while becoming a high school All-American.{{cite web|url=http://www.greatbridgewrestling.com/rootcon.php?name=history|title=Where It All Began|accessdate=2008-11-07|date=|publisher=greatbridgewrestling.com}} He earned a full [[scholarship]] to wrestle at Penn State University. At Penn State, Parker was nationally ranked as a freshman.{{cite news|url=http://docs.newsbank.com/openurl?ctx_ver=z39.88-2004&rft_id=info:sid/iw.newsbank.com:NewsBank:CDTB&rft_val_format=info:ofi/fmt:kev:mtx:ctx&rft_dat=0EB96E79B3D28A63&svc_dat=InfoWeb:aggregated5&req_dat=0D0CB579A3BDA420|title=Nittany Lions Begin Four-Match Road Trip With Loss To Illinois|accessdate=2008-12-12|date=1999-02-13|publisher=[[Newsbank]]|work=[[Centre Daily Times]]}} After transferring he continued to be ranked as a [[Redshirt (college sports)|redshirt]] junior {{convert|141|lb|kg|adj=on}} wrestler.{{cite news|url=http://docs.newsbank.com/openurl?ctx_ver=z39.88-2004&rft_id=info:sid/iw.newsbank.com:NewsBank:DLOB&rft_val_format=info:ofi/fmt:kev:mtx:ctx&rft_dat=0EFAA4D08D187F21&svc_dat=InfoWeb:aggregated5&req_dat=0D0CB579A3BDA420|title=College Wrestling Outlook - weight-by-weight (with last year's records in parentheses): |accessdate=2008-12-12|date=2001-11-07|publisher=[[Newsbank]]|work=[[The Daily Oklahoman]]}} In 2002, he placed fifth at the [[National Collegiate Athletic Association]] wrestling championships and became an All-American at Oklahoma. Following his fifth place finish, he was ranked second nationally as a redshirt senior.{{cite web|url=http://www.themat.com/rankings.php?page=ranking_details&RankingID=418|title=Amateur Wrestling News Div. I Individual Rankings|accessdate=2008-11-07|date=2002-11-30|publisher=USA Wrestling|work=Amateur Wrestling News}} In 2003, he earned an undergraduate degree in [[computer programming]] from Oklahoma. After graduating from Oklahoma, he worked as a [[computer programmer]] until he was noticed at a [[Model (person)|modeling]] convention in [[Dallas, Texas]], while accompanying a model friend. ==Acting career== Los Angeles [[talent agent]] Jon Simmons noticed Parker at the convention and talent search while Parker was waiting for his friend to finish her [[audition]]. Simmons felt Parker had "the look" and inquired about whether Parker was an actor or a model. After Parker denied both, he asked Parker to read a [[monologue]] from ''[[The Fast and the Furious (2001 film)|The Fast and The Furious]]''.{{cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2007/dec/24/entertainment/et-nate24|title=No argument, his goal is to be a star|accessdate=2008-11-08|date=2007-12-24|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|author=Welkos, Robert W.}} He then asked Parker to videotape himself doing lines from ''[[Antwone Fisher]]'' and Parker delivered a video within a week. Simmons told him to move to [[Los Angeles, California]] right away. Parker packed his clothes and computer and moved to sleep on another aspiring actor's couch. He quickly landed a part in a [[Television advertisement|television commercial]]. Bit parts and guest appearances followed. He has been working with acting coach Cary Anderson since arriving in Los Angeles. As of early 2008, Parker continued to live in [[North Hollywood, California]] with his wife, mother and three of his sisters in a {{convert|650|sqft|m2|adj=on}} one-bedroom apartment. Parker played the male lead in ''[http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0773306/ Rome and Jewel]'', a [[hip-hop]] take on ''[[Romeo and Juliet]]'' in 2006.{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/07/movies/moviesspecial/07nove.html?|title=November Releases|accessdate=2008-11-09|date=2008-09-05|publisher=[[The New York Times Company]]|work=[[The New York Times]]|author=Kehr, Dave}} He had a smaller role in ''Pride'', about an African American swim team, but one that allowed him to display his athletic physique.
