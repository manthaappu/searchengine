{{pp-semi-blp|expiry=June 1, 2011|small=yes}} {{pp-move-indef}} {{Infobox Artist Discography |Artist = [[Britney Spears]] |Image = Britney-Spears Boys.jpg|200px| |Studio = 6 |Compilation = 3 |EP = 1 |Video = 6 |Video link = Video and live albums |Singles = 30 |Music videos = 30 |Option = 3 |Option name = Specials and documentaries |Option link = Video releases |Option color = lightgray |References = Yes |Ref link = Notes }} The discography of [[Britney Spears]], an [[United States|American]] [[pop music]] singer, consists of six [[studio album]]s, three [[compilation album]]s, thirty [[single (music)|singles]] and six [[video|video albums]]. In 1997, Spears signed a recording contract with [[Jive Records|Jive]] and released her debut studio album, ''[[...Baby One More Time]]'', in January 1999. The album opened at number one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and went on to be certified fourteen-times platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]]{{cite web|url=http://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=2&table=SEARCH_RESULTS&action=&title=&artist=Britney%20Spears&format=ALBUM&debutLP=&category=&sex=&releaseDate=&requestNo=&type=&level=&label=&company=&certificationDate=&awardDescription=&catalogNo=&aSex=&rec_id=&charField=&gold=&platinum=&multiPlat=&level2=&certDate=&album=&id=&after=&before=&startMonth=1&endMonth=1&startYear=1958&endYear=2009&sort=Artist&perPage=25|title=RIAA - Gold & Platinum|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|accessdate=2009-06-25}} and three-times platinum by the [[British Phonographic Industry]].{{cite web|url=http://www.bpi.co.uk/certifiedawards/search.aspx|title=BPI Certified Awards|publisher=[[British Phonographic Industry]]|accessdate=2010-09-01}} It features the global hit single "[[...Baby One More Time (song)|...Baby One More Time]]", which notably topped the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and the [[UK Singles Chart]] and is one of the [[UK Million-Selling Singles|best-selling records of all time in Britain]], "[[(You Drive Me) Crazy]]" and the [[2000 in British music|British number-one hit]] "[[Born to Make You Happy]]".{{cite web|url=http://www.theofficialcharts.com/artist/_/britney%20spears/|title=UK Charts > Britney Spears|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|accessdate=2010-09-01}} Sixteen months later after the release of ''...Baby One More Time'', Spears released her second album, ''[[Oops!... I Did It Again]]'', which became the fastest-selling album ever by a female act in the USA with over 1.3 million copies sold in its opening week{{cite web | author=Richard Skanse | title=Oops!... She Sold 1.3 Million Albums | publisher=''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine | date=May 24, 2000 | url=http://www.rollingstone.com/artists/britneyspears/articles/story/5921422/oopsshe_sold_13_million_albums | accessdate=2009-06-25}} and was certified ten-times platinum by the RIAA and three-times platinum by the BPI. The album produced three hit singles: her third [[2000 in British music|British number-one]] "[[Oops!... I Did It Again (song)|Oops!... I Did It Again]]", "[[Lucky (Britney Spears song)|Lucky]]" and "[[Stronger (Britney Spears song)|Stronger]]". In November 2001, Spears released her [[Britney (album)|self-titled album]], which opened at the top of the ''Billboard'' 200{{cite web | author=Todd Marten | title=Teen Queen Britney Knocks King of Pop from No. 1 | publisher=Billboard magazine | date=November 15, 2001 | url=http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/search/google/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1107321 | accessdate=2009-06-25}} and produced worldwide hit "[[I'm a Slave 4 U]]", which is notable for her change of style from bubblegum pop to dance-pop and exploring into urban pop. ''[[In the Zone]]'' was released and produced major #1 hit singles like "[[Toxic (song)|Toxic]]", which earned Spears her first [[Grammy Award]],{{cite web | title=Britney Spears Wins Her First Grammy Award... | publisher=Sony Music Blog | date=February 14, 2005 | url=http://blog.sonymusic.com/sonybmg/archives/007415.html | accessdate=2009-06-25}} "[[Everytime]]", her latest [[2004 in British music|British number-one hit]] as of 2009, [[Me Against the Music]] with [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]] and the [[R. Kelly]]-produced "[[Outrageous]]". It was called by critics as her most adventurous album because of various types of genres. After four studio albums, Spears released her first [[Greatest Hits: My Prerogative|hits compilation]] in November 2004. The collection debuted at number-one on the [[Oricon|Oricon charts]]{{cite web | author=Oricon | title=Japanese Main Albums Chart | date=November 15, 2004 | url=http://jbbs.livedoor.jp/bbs/read.cgi/music/3914/1044805378/160 | accessdate=2009-06-25}} and became her highest charting album in [[Japan]]. In October 2007, Spears' highly anticipated fifth studio album, ''[[Blackout (Britney Spears album)|Blackout]]'', was released. It opened at number-two on the ''Billboard'' 200 and the [[UK Albums Chart]] and features the hit songs "[[Gimme More]]", "[[Piece of Me]]" and "[[Break the Ice (Britney Spears song)|Break the Ice]]". It was positively received by critics, who praised its innovative and modern sound. One year later, ''[[Circus (Britney Spears album)|Circus]]'', her sixth studio album, made Spears the only act in the [[Nielsen SoundScan]] era, 1991–present, to have four albums debut with 500,000 or more copies sold in the USA{{cite web | author=Katie Hasty | title=Britney's 'Circus' Debuts Atop Album Chart | work=Billboard magazine | url=http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/news/britney-s-circus-debuts-atop-album-chart-1003921402.story | date=December 10, 2008 | accessdate=2009-06-25}} and produced worldwide hits "[[Womanizer (song)|Womanizer]]", "[[Circus (song)|Circus]]", "[[If U Seek Amy]]" and "[[Radar (song)|Radar]]". In late 2009, she released new single "[[3 (song)|3]]" which was followed by her [[The Singles Collection (Britney Spears album)|second greatest hits album]]. "3" became her third number one single on the USA [[Hot 100]].
