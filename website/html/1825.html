The public wanted to believe in "wonder drugs". Unfortunately, according to Whitaker the reasons the pharmaceutical companies gave for the drugs working (too much or too little [[dopamine]] or [[serotonin]] in the patient's brain) have not proved to be true.Whitaker, part two, chapters 3, 4 and 5 ===Psychiatric drugs=== While Whitaker is pro-drug, he believes that medication must be used in a "selective, cautious manner. It should be understood that they’re not fixing any chemical imbalances. And honestly, they should be used on a short-term basis."{{cite news|author=Lipinski, Jed|title="Anatomy of an Epidemic": The hidden damage of psychiatric drugs|date=April 27, 2010|url=http://www.salon.com/books/feature/2010/04/27/interview_whitaker_anatomy_of_an_epidemic|work=Salon|publisher=Salon Media|accessdate=October 9, 2010}} He finds the idea that the "invention of [the antipsychotic] [[Chlorpromazine|Thorazine]]" emptied asylums to be a myth.{{cite journal|author=Whitaker, Robert|title=Anatomy of an Epidemic: Psychiatric Drugs and the Astonishing Rise of Mental Illness in America|url=http://www.cchr.org/sites/default/files/Anatomy_of_an_Epidemic_Psychiatric_Drugs_Rise_of_Mental_Illness.pdf|format=PDF|journal=Ethical Human Psychology and Psychiatry|publisher=Springer|page=1|issue=I|volume=7|date=Spring 2005|accessdate=October 11, 2010}} He traces the effects of what looks like an [[Iatrogenesis|iatrogenic]] epidemicWhitaker, p. 208—unfortunately, the drugs that patients receive can perturb their normal brain function.Whitaker, p. 210 {{cquote|"Psychiatrists saw that the drugs 'worked'. They saw that antipsychotics knocked down psychotic symptoms, and they observed that patients who stopped taking their medications regularly became psychotic again."Whitaker, p. 108}} In contrast, Whitaker can only offer as a "solution" the style of care documented by professor Jaakko Seikkula at Keropudas Hospital in [[Tornio]] in [[Lapland (Finland)|Lapland]] where drugs are given to patients only on a limited basis—with good outcomes. According to Whitaker, the district has the lowest per capita spending on mental health of all health districts in [[Finland]].Whitaker, p. 343 ===Children=== Whitaker sees that children are vulnerable to being prescribed a ''lifetime'' of drugs. As the author says, a psychiatrist and parents may give a child a "cocktail" to force him or her to behave.
