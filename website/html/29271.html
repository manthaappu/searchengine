'''Pimp My Search''' was a novelty website to create a [[Google]] lookalike webpage, replacing the word "Google" with a word or phrase of the user's choice.{{cite web | author=Bennett, Geoffrey | title=Has Google Finally Met Its Match? | url=http://www.npr.org/blogs/newsandviews/2008/05/has_google_finally_met_its_mat.html | work=[[NPR]] | publisher= | date=2008-05-14 | accessdate=2009-05-21}}{{cite web | author=Gill, Ronnie | title=Pimpmysearch.com offers a Google-like Web site | url=http://www.newsday.com/services/newspaper/printedition/monday/partii/ny-lshow5833924sep08a,0,4325335.story | work=[[Newsday]] | publisher= | date=2008-09-08 | accessdate=2009-05-21}} It is a non-commercial website and is not connected to Google, Inc.{{Fact|date=May 2009}} Pimp My Search was started in 2006, but became popular only in 2008 after featured in many media websites and [[YouTube]] videos.{{Fact|date=May 2009}} The site was closed down later in 2009, citing a dispute over 'trademark issues'. ==Controversies with Google== In February 2009, Pimp my Search ended Google search facilities and a posted a message that read: "Because of Trademark issues Google is no longer allowing searches from us...".http://www.pimpmysearch.com/problem.php?q= Retrieved on 2009-05-25.
