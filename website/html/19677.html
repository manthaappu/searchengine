''' James Pustejovsky''' is a TJX Feldberg [[professor]] of [[computer science]] at [[Brandeis University]] in [[Waltham, Massachusetts]]. His expertises are on Theoretical and computational modeling of language, specifically: [[Computational linguistics]], [[Lexical semantics]], [[Knowledge representation and reasoning|Knowledge representation]], temporal reasoning and [[Extraction]]. His main topic of research is [[Natural language processing]]. Pustejovsky proposed [[Generative Lexicon]] theory in [[lexical semantics]]. His other interests include [[Time|temporal]] reasoning, events, [[information extraction]], [[computational linguistics]]. == Current research == Pustejovsky's research group's current projects include the [[TimeML]] and Medstract projects. The TimeML project is attempting to create a standard [[markup language]] for [[Time|temporal]] events in a document.
