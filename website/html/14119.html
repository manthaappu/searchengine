'''Francis Lyall (Frank) Birch''' (5 December 1889 – 14 February 1956) was a British cryptographer. He was educated at [[eton college|Eton]] and [[King’s College, Cambridge]]. He was awarded an [[OBE]] in 1919 and [[Order of St Michael and St George|CMG]] in 1945. In World War I he was in the [[RNVR]], and served in the Atlantic, the Channel and the Dardanelles before joining the Naval Intelligence Division ([[Room 40]]) from 1916 to 1919. He wrote a satirical history of Room 40, ''Alice in ID25''. He was a Fellow of King’s College, Cambridge 1915-34 and a Lecturer in History at Cambridge University 1921-28. He left Cambridge for the stage in the 1930s. He joined the Naval section at [[Bletchley Park]] in September 1939, and later became Head of the (German) Naval Section.
