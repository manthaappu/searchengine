{{Orphan|date=February 2009}} {{COI|date=April 2009}} '''Phobot''' is a [[robot]] that exhibits [[behaviour]] that mimics [[fear]] and overcoming it by [[Systematic desensitization|graded exposure]]. The robot was voted the public's favourite at a conference on human-robot interaction in [[Amsterdam]]. It was designed by a team of students at the [[University of Amsterdam]], and constructed from [[Lego]].{{citeweb| url = http://www.foxnews.com/story/0,2933,338172,00.html| title = Cowardly 'Phobot' Steals Show at Amsterdam Robot Conference| publisher = [[Fox news]]| date = 17| month=March| year = 2008| author = [[Associated Press]]}} It was designed to help children deal with [[phobia]]s and [[anxiety|anxieties]]. ==Behaviour== When Phobot sees a larger robot, it retreats, and spins around, making high pitched noises, seemingly in panic. When it is gradually exposed to smaller robots, and is coached by holding its hands is talking to it in a soothing manner, it learns to overcome its fear.{{cite video |date=March 2008 |title= gaan robots mensen vervangen |url= http://www.vk.tv/video/19705 |publisher= [[Volkskrant]]}} in Dutch This approach mimics the process of systematic desensitization, or graded exposure treatment, common in the treatment of phobias. ==Design== Phobot was built for a competition at the international conference on human-robot interaction 2008 from a [[Lego Mindstorms]] robot kit, and its behaviour implemented in the Mindstorms software. It uses [[RFID]], sound and touch sensors to interact with humans.{{citeweb| url = http://staff.science.uva.nl/~hcramer/phobot/ |title = Meet Phobot! | publisher = [[University of Amsterdam]]}} It was designed as a low cost proof of concept for an affective robot, coaching children to deal with phobias and anxieties. ==External links== * [http://staff.science.uva.nl/~hcramer/phobot/ phobot homepage] * [http://hri2008.org/ Human-robot interaction conference 2008] ==References== {{Reflist}} [[Category:Robots]]
