Noelle Mckenzie Watts |website = [http://www.bake.org/ Bake VFX] }} {{Portal box|Visual Arts|Film}} '''Chris Watts''' (born June 16, 1965), is an [[United States|American]] inventor, businessman, award winning [[filmmaker]] and [[Visual effects supervisor|Visual Effects Supervisor]]. Currently the President of [http://www.bake.org/ Bake Visual Effects, Inc.] located in [[Los Angeles, California|Los Angeles]], [[California]], he began his film career with [http://www.facebook.com/group.php?v=wall&gid=87686098648 Dream Quest Images] in 1989. Watts originated, invented and implemented the [[Digital intermediate]] process in 1995 when filming the [[New Line Cinema]] [[feature film|feature]], [[Pleasantville (film)|''Pleasantville'' (1998)]].http://www.studiodaily.com/filmandvideo/people/visions/Peter-Doyle-and-the-Joy-of-Grading_4501.html Later in 2004, he also pioneered the use of [[Digital single-lens reflex camera|digital still cameras]] as a high resolution replacement for film cameras on [[Tim Burton]]'s [[stop motion|stop animation]], [[Corpse Bride|''The Corpse Bride'']].{{cite web|last=Watts |first=Chris |url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0915121/bio |title=Biography for Chris Watts |publisher=imdb.com |date= |accessdate=2010-07-07}} Watts gained wide recognition with the 2007 box office hit, ''[[300 (film)|''300'']]'', a [[Warner Bros.]] [[action film]] adapted from the graphic novel [[300 (comics)|of the same name]] by writer-artist [[Frank Miller (comics)|Frank Miller]] of [[Dark Horse Comics]] [[miniseries]]. He later went on supervising the 2009 [[fantasy film]], [[Where the Wild Things Are (film)|''Where the Wild Things Are'']].
