In [[coding theory]], '''Srivastava codes''' form a class of parameterised [[Error detection and correction|error-correcting codes]] which are a special case of [[alternant code]]s. ==Definition== The original ''Srivastava code'' over GF(''q'') of length ''n'' is defined by a parity check matrix ''H'' of [[alternant matrix|alternant]] form :\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\alpha_1^\mu}{\alpha_1-w_1} & \cdots & \frac{\alpha_n^\mu}{\alpha_n-w_1} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\alpha_1^\mu}{\alpha_1-w_s} & \cdots & \frac{\alpha_n^\mu}{\alpha_n-w_s} \\ \end{bmatrix} where the α''i'' and ''z''''i'' are elements of GF(''q''''m'') ==Properties== The parameters of this code are length ''n'', dimension ≥ ''n'' − ''m''s and minimum distance ≥ s + 1. == References == * {{cite book | author=F.J.
