{|{{Infobox Aircraft Begin | name=Meggitt BTT-3 Banshee | image=Image:Meggitt Banshees.jpg | caption=A team of [[Royal Brunei Armed Forces]] military and civilian contractors prepare "Banshee" unmanned drones for launch from the U.S. Navy's dock landing ship [[USS Fort McHenry]] (LSD 43). }}{{Infobox Aircraft Type | type=Target Drone | national origin=United Kingdom | manufacturer=[[Meggitt]] | designer= | first flight=1983 | introduced=1984 | retired= | status= | primary user= | more users= | produced= | number built=Over 5000 {{cite web|title=Banshee|url=http://www.meggitttrainingsystems.com/main.php?id=46|accessdate=7 Jan 2011}} | program cost= | unit cost= | developed from= | variants with their own articles= }} |} The '''Meggitt BTT-3 Banshee''' is a traget drone developed in the 1980s for air defense systems training. ==Development== The Banshee was first developed by [[Target Technology Ltd.]]{{cite web|title=Target Drones|url=http://www.vectorsite.net/twuav_03.html|accessdate=7 Jan 2011}} ==Design== The Banshee is a built mostly out of composite material with a delta wing planform. It is designed to float for overseas operations recovery. Installable options include, radar enhancement devices, flare or chaff dispenser, and target sleeves. It can simulate a sea skimming missle or serve as a reconasance uav with a camera. ==Operational history== The Banshee has been deployed in over 40 Countries.{{cite web|title=Banshee|url=http://www.meggitttrainingsystems.com/main.php?id=46|accessdate=7 Jan 2011}} It has been tested against Blowpipe, Chaparral, Crotale, Javelin, Phalanx, Rapier and Sea Sparrow systems.{{cite web|title=Meggitt BTT-3 Banshee|url=http://www.janes.com/articles/Janes-Unmanned-Aerial-Vehicles-and-Targets/Meggitt-BTT-3-Banshee-United-Kingdom.html|accessdate=7 Jan 2011}}
