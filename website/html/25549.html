{{about|NAND Logic in the sense of building other logic gates using just NAND gates|NAND Gates|NAND gate|NAND in the purely logical sense|Logical NAND|logic gates generally|Logic gate}} {{Unreferenced|date=December 2009}} [[NAND gate]]s are one of the two basic [[logic gates]] (the other being [[NOR logic]]) from which any other logic gates can be built. Due to this property, NAND and [[NOR gate]]s are sometimes called "universal gates". However, modern [[integrated circuit]]s are not constructed exclusively from a single type of gate. Instead, [[Electronic design automation|EDA]] tools are used to convert the description of a logical circuit to a [[netlist]] of complex gates ([[standard cell]]s) or transistors ([[full custom]] approach). ==[[NOT]]== A NOT gate is made by joining the inputs of a NAND gate.
