{{unreferenced|date=October 2010}} An '''instruction window''' in the field of [[superscalar]] [[CPU]] architecture is a group of [[Instruction (computer science)|instructions]] actively processed in parallel. Also the size or maximum size of either such a group or its containing facility can be meant (size being measured in number of instructions). Instructions which, in [[out-of-order execution]], are put aside into a [[shelving buffer]] or are waiting in an [[instruction queue]] are not thought of being inside of an instruction window. [[Instruction pipeline|Pipelined instructions]] in different states of processing are not in the same instruction window. Both concepts might be visualized as being orthogonal to each other.
