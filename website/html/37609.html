'''This Week in Blackness''' (also referred to as ''TWiB!'') is an American culture [[blog]] which primarily publishes news, pop culture and opinion from a Black progressive point of view. Founded in 2008 during the presidential campaign season it is also the home of the video series [http://youtube.com/bccofilms BcCo Films: Elon James White] of the same name hosted by [[Elon James White]]. The Blog was nominated for 4 [[Black Weblog Awards]] in 2009 and won 3 [http://www.blackweblogawards.com/past-winners/#2009 Black Weblog 2009 Winners] including "Blog of the Year." This Week in Blackness originally published the controversial ''"Notes From a Phantom Negro: Skip Gates, Please Sit Down"''[http://thisweekinblackness.com/2009/07/23/notes-from-a-phantom-negro-skip-gatesplease-sit-down/ Notes From a Phantom Negro: Skip Gates, Please Sit Down] ''This Week In Blackness'' July 23rd, 2009 discussing the arrest of Henry Louis Gates Jr. which was then republished by [[Salon.com]] [http://www.salon.com/news/opinion/feature/2009/07/24/gates Skip Gates: Please Sit Down] ''Salon.Com'' July 27th, 2009 The blog also originally posted "Message From The Average Black Person"[http://thisweekinblackness.com/2009/06/24/a-message-from-the-average-black-person/ Message From the Average Black Person] ''This Week In Blackness'' June 24th, 2009 which later was published by [[The Huffington Post]][http://www.huffingtonpost.com/elon-james-white/a-message-from-the-averag_b_220022.html Message From The Average Black Person] ''The Huffington Post'', June 24th, 2009 and [[AlterNet]]. The site is best known for the critically acclaimed web series by the same name.
