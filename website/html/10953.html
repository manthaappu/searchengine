[[image:Donald Becker.jpg|right|thumbnail|]] '''Donald Becker''' is a notable [[programmer|developer]] well known for writing many of the [[Ethernet]] drivers for the [[Linux]] operating system. Thousands of computers around the world routinely use his drivers to connect to the [[Internet]]. Becker, in collaboration with [[Thomas Sterling (computing)|Thomas Sterling]], created [[Beowulf (computing)|Beowulf]] clustering while at [[NASA]], using software to connect many inexpensive PCs to solve complex math problems typically reserved for classic [[supercomputer]]s. For this work, Becker received the [[Gordon Bell Prize]] in 1997.
