[[File:PKahun LV2.jpg|thumb|300px|Fragments of the Kahun Papyrus on [[veterinary]] medicine]] The '''Kahun Papyri (KP)''' are a collection of ancient Egyptian texts discussing administrative, mathematical and medical topics. Its many fragments were discovered by [[Flinders Petrie]] in 1889 and are kept at the [[University College London]]. This collection of papyri is one of the largest ever found. Most of the texts are dated to ca. 1825 BC, to the reign of [[Amenemhat III]]. In general the collection spans the [[Middle Kingdom of Egypt]]. [http://www.digitalegypt.ucl.ac.uk/lahun/papyri.html Lahun Papyrus Collection at University College, London] The texts span a variety of topics: * Business papers of the cult of [[Senusret II]] * Hymns to king [[Senusret III]]. [http://www.digitalegypt.ucl.ac.uk/lahun/kinghymns.html Hymns to king Senusret III] from University College, London * The [[Kahun Gynecological Papyrus|Kahun Gynaecological Papyrus]], which deals with [[gynaecology|gynaecological]] illnesses and conditions.
