'''Roland Trimen''' [[Royal Society|FRS]] (October 29, 1840 [[London]] — July 25, 1916 [[London]]) was a [[United Kingdom|British]]-[[South Africa]]n [[entomologist]], best-known for ''South African Butterflies'' (1887-89), a collaborative work with Colonel [[James Henry Bowker]]. Trimen arrived at the Cape in 1859, taking up a post in the Auditor-General's office in [[Cape Town]] and then transferred to the office of the Colonial Secretary. In 1872 he became curator of the [[South African Museum]] in Cape Town, succeeding [[Edgar Leopold Layard]]. He attended the international congress on [[Phylloxera]] in [[Bordeaux]] in 1880. Failing health caused him to resign his position at the South African Museum in 1893. Trimen won the [[Darwin Medal]] of the [[Royal Society]] in 1910 and was the director of the [[South African Museum]] of [[Cape Town]].
