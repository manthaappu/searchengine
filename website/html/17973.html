An '''imaging spectrometer''' is an instrument used in the field of [[imaging spectroscopy]] to acquire a spectrally-resolved image of an object or scene, often referred to as a [[datacube]] due to the three-dimensional representation of the data. Namely, two axes of the image corresponds to distance and the third to [[wavelength]]. The principle of operation is the same as that of the simple [[spectrometer]], but special care is taken to avoid [[optical aberration]]s for better image quality.
