I, Avatar | awards = [[Ars Electronica Golden Nica]]: | website = http://www.markmeadows.com }} '''Mark Stephen Meadows''' is an American author and artist. In addition to his illustration, books, and travelogues he also develops software. He is the co-inventor of several US patents relating to [[artificial intelligence]] and [[Avatar (computing)|avatars]], and he lectures internationally on this work.[http://www.mediamatic.net/article-32199-en.html Avatars As Self-Portraits], Mediamatic Lecture, Amsterdam, 2008.[https://www.cmpevents.com/GD07/a.asp?option=G&V=3&id=518969 Emotional User Interface and NPCs], Game Developers Conference, Los Angeles, 2007.[http://2007.sxsw.com/interactive/programming/panels/?action=bio&id=129953 Avatars and AI], SXSW Lecture, Austin, 2007.[http://youtube.com/watch?v=sH9eiLbGKGg Artificial Intelligence and World Travel], XMedia Lecture, Singapore, 2006.[http://lamp.edu.au/2005/12/20/podcast-interactive-narratives/ Interactive Narrative], AFTRS Lecture, Sydney, 2005. Meadows is known for his hitchhiking adventures,[http://www.digihitch.com/road-culture/hitchhikers-modern/950 Modern Hitchhikers], 1976 - present. specifically for visiting Baghdad in 2003, and his interviews with terrorists in Sri Lanka. He holds a [[United States Coast Guard|USCG]] captain's license. ==Career== Since 1987, Meadows has been selling his artwork to galleries and museums throughout the United States and Europe. He has received awards from [[Ars Electronica]],"Difference Engine #3" awarded to Construct. [http://www.aec.at/en/archives/prix_archive/prix_projekt.asp?iProjectID=2171# Ars Electronica, Golden Nica], Prix 1999, Interactive Art category.''www.aec.at'' and the [[Cooper-Hewitt Museum|Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum]], among others. In 1993 Meadows moved to San Francisco where he helped design [[WELL (virtual community)|WELL.com]] and he also helped develop the first open-protocol 3D multi-user environment in 1995.Early virtual world work; article: "How To Build A Metaverse" [http://www.newscientist.com/article/mg14819994.000-how-to-build-a-metaverse.html New Scientist], 14 October 1995 He worked as researcher and artist at [[Xerox Parc|Xerox-PARC]], as Creative Director for a [[Stanford Research Institute]] venture, and as creative director and co-founder of a VR and Internet company called Construct. In 2001 he opened a gallery in [[Paris]] where he sold his paintings for two years and then spent time in the [http://www.waag.org Waag Society] in [[Holland]] as an artist/researcher. In 2005 and 2007 he founded two companies dedicated to [[artificial intelligence]] and [[Avatar (computing)|avatars]].
