{{Infobox person | name = Daniel Robbins | honorific_suffix = | image = | image_size = | alt = | caption = | birth_date = | birth_place = [[Montreal]], {{flag|Quebec}} | residence = [[Albuquerque, New Mexico]] | nationality = {{flag|United States}} | other_names = | education = [[Computer science]] | alma_mater = [[University of New Mexico]] | occupation = | years_active = | employer = [[OPNET]] | organization = [[Funtoo Technologies]] | known_for = [[Free Software]], [[Hacker (programmer subculture)|Hacker]], [[Gentoo Linux|Gentoo]], [[Funtoo]] | notable_works = | home_town = | awards = | module = | module2 = | module3 = | module4 = | module5 = | module6 = | website = {{URL|www.funtoo.org}} | footnotes = | box_width = }} '''Daniel Robbins''' (US citizen, born in Montreal, Quebec{{fr}} [http://www.funtoo-quebec.org/node/19 Funtoo-Quebec.org source]) is a [[computer programmer]] and consultant best known as the founder and former chief architect of the [[Gentoo Linux]] project. In 2008, he launched the [[Funtoo|Funtoo project]], a [[free software|free]] [[GNU/Linux distribution]] based on Gentoo, and is the project's lead hacker and organizer. He also currently works at the same time in [[Albuquerque, New Mexico]], as engineer at [[OPNET]], and as president for [[Funtoo Technologies]]. ==Biography== === Background === During his time as a system administrator at the [[University of New Mexico]] in [[Albuquerque]] Robbins first came into contact with Linux. He became a developer on [[Stampede Linux]], and later formed his own distribution ''Enoch Linux'', which was later renamed [[Gentoo Linux|Gentoo]] in 2002. ===Free Software=== ==== Gentoo Linux ==== The [[Gentoo Linux]] distribution created enormous interest almost immediately. It remains by far{{Citation needed|date=September 2010}} the most high-profile [[:Category:Source-based Linux distributions|source-based Linux distribution]], and the quality of its documentation, and friendliness of its forums have been widely praised.{{Citation needed|date=September 2010}} ====Gentoo Foundation==== However, like many other [[free software]] projects, Gentoo struggled to create a [[business model]] which would support its key developers.{{cite web|first= Eugenia |last= Loli-Queru |authorlink= Eugenia Loli-Queru |title= Interview with Gentoo's Daniel Robbins |url= http://www.osnews.com/story.php?news_id=1080 |archiveurl= |publisher= [[OSNews]] |doi= |date= 2002-05-14 |archivedate= |accessdate= 2009-04-17 |quote= I very much want to find a way to turn the Gentoo Linux project into a profitable enterprise. ====Funtoo Linux==== In 2008, Robbins began to work on [[Funtoo]], a project created to allow him to work on extending the technologies originally created for Gentoo.http://www.funtoo.org/en/funtoo/about/ ===Technical writer=== He has also been a fairly prolific [[technical writer]], writing well-regarded tutorials for [[International Business Machines|IBM]] and the Gentoo project, among others. ===Professional experience=== ==== Microsoft ==== Robbins' move to [[Microsoft]], on 13 June 2005, attracted attention{{cite web|url=http://linux.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=05/06/13/2137239&tid=109 |title=Slashdot | Gentoo Founder on his way to Redmond |publisher=Linux.slashdot.org |date= |accessdate=2009-08-04}}{{cite web|last=Marson |first=Ingrid |url=http://news.com.com/Gentoo+Linux+founder+to+educate+Microsoft/2100-7344_3-5749482.html |title=Gentoo Linux founder to 'educate' Microsoft | CNET News.com |publisher=News.com.com |date=2005-06-16 |accessdate=2009-08-04}} within the Linux community, which has historically had a combative relationship with Microsoft. He described his role working for [[Bill Hilf]] as "...helping Microsoft to understand Open Source and community-based projects..."{{cite web|url=http://www.osnews.com/story.php?news_id=10838 |title=Gentoo Founder Joins Microsoft |publisher=Osnews.com |date= |accessdate=2009-08-04}} However, Robbins resigned less than a year later on 16 January 2006 due to frustrations that he was unable to fully utilize his technical skills in this position.
