{{Lowercase|title=q-Vandermonde identity}} In [[mathematics]], in the field of [[combinatorics]], the '''q-Vandermonde identity''' is the [[q-analogue]] of the [[Chu-Vandermonde identity]] : \binom{m + n}{k}_q = \sum_{j} \binom{m}{k - j}_q \binom{n}{j}_q q^{j(m-k+j)}. The proof follows from observing the [[q-binomial]] identity with ''q''-commuting operators (namely ''BA'' = ''qAB''). ==Other conventions== In the conventions common in applications to [[quantum groups]], where a different ''q''-binomial coefficient is used, which is symmetric under exchanging q and q^{-1}. Denoting it by B(n,k;q) the ''q''-Vandermonde identity reads : B(m + n,k;q) = q^{m k} \sum_{j=0}^{n} q^{-(m+n)j} B(m,k - j;q) B(n,j;q). ==Proof== Assume that ''A'' and ''B'' are operators that ''q''-commute: : BA = qAB.\, Then: : (A + B)^m(A + B)^n \, : = \left(\sum_{i=0}^m \binom{m}{i}_{q}A^{i}B^{m-i}\right) \left(\sum_{j=0}^n \binom{n}{j}_{q}A^{j}B^{n-j}\right) :: = \sum_{i,j} \binom{m}{i}_q \binom{n}{j}_q A^{i}B^{m-i}A^{j}B^{n-j} :: = \sum_{i,j} \binom{m}{i}_q \binom{n}{j}_q q^{j(m-i)}A^{i+j}B^{m+n-i-j}. This makes use of the fact that BA^2 = BAA = qABA = q^2AAB = q^2A^2B. \, Now, consider the coefficient of A^{k}B^{m+n-k}\, in this expression. This gives \sum_{j} \binom{m}{k-j}_q \binom{n}{j}_q q^{j(m-k+j)}. Now, from the [[q-binomial]] theory, we recognize that (A+B)^m(A+B)^n=(A+B)^{m+n}\, And thus, the coefficient of A^{k}B^{m+n-k}\, is \binom{m+n}{k}_q. Combining the results gives : \binom{m + n}{k}_q = \sum_{j} \binom{m}{k - j}_q \binom{n}{j}_q q^{j(m-k+j)}. \mathrm{QED}\, {{Unreferenced|date=August 2010}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Q-Vandermonde Identity}} [[Category:Number theory]] [[Category:Q-analogs|*]] [[Category:Mathematical identities]] {{combin-stub}}
