[[University of Groningen|Groningen]], [[University of Montpellier|Montpellier]], [[University of Chile|Chile]], [[University of Alicante|Alicante]] |religion = |signature = }} '''R. Tyrrell Rockafellar''' is one of the world's leaders in [[mathematical optimization|optimization theory]] and related fields of [[mathematical analysis|analysis]] and [[oriented matroid|combinatorics]]. He is professor emeritus at the departments of [[mathematics]] and [[applied mathematics]] at the [[University of Washington, Seattle]]. ==Prizes== Rockafellar received the [[John von Neumann Theory Prize]] from the [[Institute for Operations Research and Management Science]] and delivered the 1993 von Neumann lecture for the [[Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics]] (SIAM). Rockafellar and his coauthor [[Roger J-B Wets]] were awarded the [[Frederick W. Lanchester Prize]] for 1997 by [[Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences]] (INFORMS). The [[Institute for Scientific Information]] (ISI) lists Rockafellar as a [[Institute for Scientific Information highly cited researcher|highly cited researcher]].
