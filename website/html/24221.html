[[John Giannandrea]], [[Chief technical officer|CTO]] | industry = [[Data management]] | products = Freebase — an online database | num_employees = | homepage = [http://www.metaweb.com www.metaweb.com] }} '''Metaweb Technologies, Inc.''' was a United States company based in [[San Francisco, CA|San Francisco]] that developed [[Freebase (database)|Freebase]], described as an "open, shared database of the world's knowledge". The company was founded by [[Danny Hillis]] in July, 2005, and operated in [[stealth mode]] until 2007. '''Metaweb''' was acquired by [[Google]] in July, 2010 {{cite press release |publisher=Metaweb |date=2010-07-16 |url=http://blog.freebase.com/2010/07/16/metaweb-joins-google/ |format= |title=Metaweb joins Google |accessdate=2010-07-16 }}. Although Metaweb no longer exists as a separate corporate entity, Freebase and its associated website [http://www.freebase.com freebase.com] continue to be provided as an open database under Metaweb's original [[CC-BY]] licensing terms. ==Funding== On March 14, 2006, Metaweb received $15 million in funding.
