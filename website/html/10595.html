'''Direct''' or '''quoted speech''' is a [[sentence (linguistics)|sentence]] (or several sentences) that reports speech or thought in its original form, as phrased by the first speaker.{{cite web|url=http://www.sil.org/linguistics/GlossaryOflinguisticTerms/WhatIsDirectSpeech.htm|title=What is direct speech?|publisher=SIL International|work=Glossary of linguistic terms|last=Loos|first=Eugene E.|coauthors=Susan Anderson; Dwight H. Day, Jr.; Paul C. Jordan; J. Douglas Wingate|accessdate=2010-06-20}} It is usually enclosed in [[quotation mark]]s. The cited speaker is either mentioned in the ''inquit'' ([[Latin language|Latin]] "he says") or implied. ==Examples== * He said, "It's raining". * "It's raining," he thought. * "It's cold outside," he said, "and it's starting to rain". * "How is the weather?" — "It's cold outside." — "Yes, and it's starting to rain." ===Comparison between direct, indirect and free indirect speech=== {{Comparison direct and indirect speech}} ==References== {{reflist}} [[Category:Semantic units]] [[Category:Syntactic entities]] {{syntax-stub}} [[de:Direkte Rede]] [[es:Estilo directo]] [[fr:Discours direct]] [[gl:Estilo directo]] [[is:Bein ræða]] [[nl:Directe rede]] [[ru:Прямая речь]] [[uk:Пряма мова]]
