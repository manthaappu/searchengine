{{morefootnotes|date=December 2008}} '''Tim Sweeney''', born in 1970,http://www.gamasutra.com/view/feature/4035/from_the_past_to_the_future_tim_.php is a [[computer game]] [[game programmer|programmer]] and the founder of [[Epic Games]], being best known for his work on ''[[ZZT]]'' and the [[Unreal Engine]]. Sweeney established Epic as a [[shareware]] company while he was a student majoring in [[mechanical engineering]] at the [[University of Maryland, College Park|University of Maryland]]. Sweeney said that he had been interested in game development and computer programming since he was 10 years old.http://www.gamasutra.com/view/feature/4035/from_the_past_to_the_future_tim_.php?page=2 Sweeney finally started making games out of his parents' basement where he lived.http://www.gamasutra.com/view/feature/4035/from_the_past_to_the_future_tim_.php?page=3 Epic got its start when Sweeney created ''[[ZZT]]'', the company's first game. As Sweeney said in interviews, ''ZZT'''s success fueled the early growth of his company. Sweeney worked on Epic's [[Unreal Engine]], the [[game engine]] used for the [[Unreal (series)|Unreal series]] of [[first-person shooter]]s and other computer games.{{cite book | title=High score!: the illustrated history of electronic games | series=Computer Games | first1=Rusel | last1=DeMaria | first2=Johnny L. | last2=Wilson | publisher=McGraw-Hill Professional | year=2003 | isbn=0072231726 | page=300 }}Tim Sweeney is the "co-founder of Epic Games and the brains behind every iteration of the widely licensed Unreal series of 3D game engines" [http://arstechnica.com/gaming/news/2008/09/gpu-sweeney-interview.ars]. In 2005, Sweeney attended Wake Tech Community College Digital Game Expo as a guest speaker.
