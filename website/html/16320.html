{{refimprove|date=October 2010}} '''Harold W. Thimbleby''' (born 19 July 1955) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[professor]] of [[computer science]] at [[Swansea University]], [[Wales]]. He is known for his works on [[user interface design]] within the realm of [[human computer interaction]].{{fact|date=October 2010}} == Overview == Harold Thimbleby held the post of director of UCLIC, [[University College London]]'s Interaction Centre, from its establishment in 2001. From 2001 to 2004, he was also the 28th [[Gresham Professors of Geometry|Professor of Geometry]] at [[Gresham College]], [[London]]. Thimbleby founded the Future Interaction Technology Lab[http://www.fitlab.eu/ Future Interaction Technology Lab]. at Swansea University in 2005.{{fact|date=October 2010}} Thimbleby runs the Swansea University Research Forum, as well as giving talks on science and religion.{{fact|date=October 2010}} ==Research interests== Thimbleby's research interests include: * Interactive handwriting calculators[http://www.cs.swan.ac.uk/calculators/ Calculators], Swansea University, UK.Lucy Sherriff, ''[http://www.theregister.co.uk/2005/06/28/super_calculators/ Super calculators make maths super-easy]'', [http://www.theregister.co.uk/ The Register], UK, 28 June 2005. * Improving medical devices [http://cs.swan.ac.uk/~csharold/health/] * Improving ethics in research [http://www.cs.swan.ac.uk/~csharold/ethics/index.html] * Markov Modeling [http://www.cs.swan.ac.uk/~csharold/Markov/index.html] * [[Esperanto]] for the masses * Matrix Modeling [http://www.cs.swan.ac.uk/~csharold/matrixweb/index.html] ==Selected works== * Article on [[literate programming]],1986 (Winner of the [[British Computer Society]] Wilkes Award.{{fact|date=October 2010}}) * ''User Interface Design'', [[Addison-Wesley]], 1990. * ''HyperProgramming'', with G.
