{{Orphan|date=February 2009}} '''Microlinguistics''' is a branch of [[linguistics]] that concerns itself with the study of [[language]] systems in the abstract, without regard to the [[Meaning (linguistics)|meaning]] or notional content of linguistic expressions. In micro-linguistics, language is reduced to the abstract mental elements of [[syntax]] and [[phonology]]. It [[contrast]]s with [[macro-linguistics]], which includes meanings, and especially with [[sociolinguistics]], which studies how language and meaning function within human social systems.{{Citation | last = Matthews | first = P.H. | author-link = | publication-date = | date = | year = 2002 | title = The Concise Oxford Dictionary of Linguistics | periodical = | series = | publication-place = Oxford | place = | publisher = Oxford UP | volume = | issue = | pages = | url = | issn = | pmid= | pmc= | doi = | oclc = | accessdate = }}.
