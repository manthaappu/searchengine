[[Image:Columbia Supercomputer - NASA Advanced Supercomputing Facility.jpg|thumb|right|290px|The Columbia Supercomputer at NASA's Advanced Supercomputing Facility at [[Ames Research Center]]]] Named in honor of the crew who died in the [[Space Shuttle Columbia disaster|''Columbia'' disaster]], '''Columbia''' is a [[supercomputer]] built by [[Silicon Graphics]] for [[NASA]]. Its main purpose was to simulate the violent collision and merger of spiral galaxies that lead to the formation of elliptical galaxies. It is connected to the [[NASA Research and Engineering Network]] and was installed at the [[NASA Advanced Supercomputing facility]] in 2004. According to the [[TOP500]] list of the fastest supercomputers, it entered the list in November 2004 at position 2, {{cite web |url=http://www.top500.org/system/ranking/7288 |title=November 2006 - TOP500 Supercomputing Sites |publisher=[[TOP500]] |accessdate=2007-05-22 }} running at 51.87 [[FLOPS|teraflops]], or 51.87 trillion [[floating point]] calculations per second. By June 2007 it had dropped to position 13, and by June 2008 was at position 25.[http://www.top500.org/list/2008/06/100 TOP500 List - June 2008 (1-100) | TOP500 Supercomputing Sites] It is composed of twenty [[Altix|SGI Altix 3000]] nodes running [[SUSE Linux Enterprise Server]] 9 {{cite web |url=http://www.computerwire.com/industries/research/?pid=4BB10C99-8E6D-4804-81D0-4062B05B5B7D |title=11 September 2007: NASA Buys Big Xeon-Linux Cluster from SGI |publisher=computerwire |accessdate=2008-01-31 }} each of which has 512 [[Intel Corporation|Intel]] [[Itanium 2]] [[central processing unit|processor]]s bringing the total number of processors to 10,240. It has 20 terabytes of memory (Provided by [[Dataram]]), 440 terabytes of storage, and 10 petabytes of archive storage.{{cite web |date=2007-01-31 |title=Columbia System Facts |publisher=NASA |url=http://www.nas.nasa.gov/Resources/Systems/columbia.html |accessdate=2007-03-27 }} The SGI Altix platform was selected due to a positive experience with [[Kalpana (supercomputer)|Kalpana]], a single Altix 512-CPU system operated by NASA Ames which was integrated into the Columbia supercomputer system. The computers are connected together with a Voltaire [[InfiniBand]] ISR 9288 288 port [[network switch|switch]] with transfer speeds of up to 10 [[gigabit]]s (or 12500 [[megabyte]]s) per second, [[10 Gigabit Ethernet]] and multiple 1 gigabit Ethernet nodes. == References == {{reflist}} == External links == {{commons|Columbia (supercomputer)}} *[http://www.nas.nasa.gov/About/Projects/Columbia/columbia.html NASA Columbia homepage] *[http://www.top500.org/system/7288 TOP500 entry] *[http://www.sgi.com/company_info/newsroom/press_releases/2004/october/worlds_fastest.html Press release from SGI] *[http://www.nasa.gov/home/hqnews/2004/oct/HQ_04353_columbia.txt Press release from NASA] *[http://memory.dataram.com/ Dataram Memory] [[Category:Supercomputers]] [[Category:One-of-a-kind computers]] {{super-compu-stub}} [[ar:كولومبيا (حاسوب فائق)]] [[de:Columbia (Supercomputer)]] [[fr:Columbia (superordinateur)]] [[is:Columbia (ofurtölva)]] [[it:Columbia (supercomputer)]] [[ja:Columbia]] [[pl:Columbia (komputer)]] [[fi:Columbia (supertietokone)]]
