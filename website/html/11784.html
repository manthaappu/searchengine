The essays were selected from his monthly column "The View of Life" in ''[[Natural History (magazine)|Natural History]]'' magazine, to which Gould contributed for 27 years. The book deals, in typically discursive fashion, with themes familiar to Gould's writing: evolution and its teaching, science biography, probabilities and common sense. The title essay, [http://www.stephenjaygould.org/library/gould_eight-piggies.html "Eight Little Piggies"], explores concepts such as [[archetypes]] and [[polydactyly]] via the anatomy of early [[tetrapods]]. Other essays discuss themes such as the scale of extinction, vertebrate anatomy, grand [[evolution|patterns of evolution]], and [[human nature]]. ==Contents==
