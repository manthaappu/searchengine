Ivannikov at annual plenary conference at [[MIPT]], November 27, 2009 |workplaces = [[Institute for System Programming]] |alma_mater = [[Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology|MIPT]] }} '''Viktor Petrovich Ivannikov''' ({{lang-ru|Виктор Петрович Иванников}}; born February 27, 1940) is a [[Russia]]n computer scientist, member of [[Russian Academy of Sciences]], head of System Programming Chair, [[Institute for System Programming]]. ==Scientific achievements== Interests: system programming, computer architecture, operating systems. Viktor Ivannikov worked for [[Lebedev Institute of Precision Mechanics and Computer Engineering]] from 1962 to 1980, starting as a computer technician, leaving as a distinguished engineer, where he developed [[D-68 (operating system)|D-68]] operating system for [[BESM|BESM-6]] computer.
