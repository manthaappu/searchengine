{{expert-subject|Computers|date=November 2009}} {{Orphan|date=February 2009}} {{Infobox Software|name=Handel|developer=Ian Holmes ([[UC Berkeley]])|latest_release_version=1|operating_system=[[UNIX]], [[Linux]], [[Apple Macintosh|Mac]], [[Cygwin]] on [[Windows XP]]|genre=Bioinformatics tool|licence=Open source|website=[http://biowiki.org/HANDEL Handel homepage]}} '''Handel''' is a suite of programs for [[multiple sequence alignment]]. Since it is based on a [[Models of DNA evolution|model of DNA sequence evolution]] that explicitly incorporates [[Indel|insertion and deletion events]], it effectively doubles as a program for [[ancestral reconstruction|ancestral sequence reconstruction]]. The theory underpinning '''Handel''' is based on the [[finite state transducer]], a concept borrowed from computational linguistics. '''Handel''' can be downloaded as part of the [http://biowiki.org/DART DART software package]. It accepts input files in [http://www.cgb.ki.se/cgb/groups/sonnhammer/Stockholm.html Stockholm] format. ==References== * Holmes I, Bruno WJ.
