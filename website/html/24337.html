}} '''Michael Simms''' is the creator of the Tux Games website and the founder of [[Linux Game Publishing]]. == Biography == In 2000 — 2001 he worked in Wcities as Lead product architect.[http://uk.linkedin.com/pub/michael-simms/12/74/828 linkedin, Michael Simms’s Experience] He was a beta tester Loki’s [[Civilization: Call to Power]] and [[Descent 3]]. [http://www.mobygames.com/game/linux/descent_/credits, MobyGames, Descent 3 - Linux beta testers] In response to being unable to order a version of [[Loki Software]]'s port of Civilization: Call to Power from any British reseller, Michael created the Tux Games website, an on-line Linux games store.[http://www.techradar.com/news/gaming/bringing-windows-games-to-linux-622659?artc_pg=1 TechRadar, Bringing Windows games to Linux] The website went on-line ten seconds after midnight on January 1, 2000, selling its first title on January 3.[http://blog.linuxgamepublishing.com/2009/05/15/lgp-history-pt-1-how-lgp-came-to-be/ Linux Game Publishing Blog, LGP History pt 1: How LGP came to be] In response to Loki's demise and the effect it would have on Tux Games, [[Linux Game Publishing]] was founded on October 15, 2001 to port and publish new games for [[Linux]].
