The college provides [[secondary education]] and courses for the community. It is the only such college/school in the town of Exmouth. The college has specialist status in Maths and Computing and is equipped with 900 workstations many of which are in one of the 12 IT suites. The principal is Mr A K Alexander. The college has a website that is updated daily which has information on forthcoming events as well as a parents' guide and news and photo archive. The web site includes the college on-line learning platform. The school has two sites, on Gipsy Lane and Green Close, connected by a bridge and pathway. The Green Close site is primarily for the 'lower school' (years 7-9) while the Gipsy Lane site caters for the 'upper school' (years 10-11) and sixth form (post-16). Both sites have undergone rapid development, with the Turner Building (1997) and Pauline Hitchings Building (2005) opening on the Green Close site and Judith Telfer Centre on the Gipsy Lane site (1997).The school has the largest number of students in England's West Country (2,327 pupils including 6th formhttp://www.ofsted.gov.uk/oxedu_reports/display/(id)/70764 == Subjects offered == The most recent [[OFSTED]] inspection graded the college curriculum as 'outstanding' and the college as a whole as " Good". The PE Dept is graded "Outstanding" As well as offering the core curriculum with a choice between academic and vocational subjects, the college also offers an alternative curriculum, focusing entirely on workplace skills. The options available at KS 4 are extensive. The College has Mathemetics and ICT as its specialist subjects. The school has a lunch time league available to all year 10 and 11 students. Fixtures are held every lunchtime by the p.e. teachers and events are organised by Mr Taylor. == Specialist status == The school has specialist status in Maths and ICT.
