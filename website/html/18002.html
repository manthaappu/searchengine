Under the [[Meiji Constitution]], no one was permitted to use the Imperial Seal except the [[Emperor of Japan]], who used a 16 petal chrysanthemum with sixteen tips of another row of petals showing behind the first row. Therefore, each member of the Imperial family used a slightly modified version of the seal. [[Shinto]] shrines either displayed the imperial seal or incorporated elements of the seal into their own emblems. Earlier in Japanese history, when [[Emperor Go-Daigo]], who tried to break the power of the shogunate 1333, was exiled, he adopted the seventeen petal chrysanthemum to differentiate himself from his successor, Emperor Kōgon, who kept the emperial 16 petal ''mon''. The symbol is a yellow or orange [[chrysanthemum]] with black or red outlines and background. A central disc is surrounded by a front set of 16 petals. A rear set of 16 petals are half staggered in relation to the front set and are visible at the edges of the flower. An example of the chrysanthemum being used is in the badge for the [[Order of the Chrysanthemum]].
