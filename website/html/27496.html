[http://openhumanitiespress.org/ '''Open Humanities Press'''] is an international open access publishing initiative in the humanities, specializing in critical and cultural theory. OHP's editorial board includes leading scholars and open access advocates such as [[Alain Badiou]], [[Jonathan Culler]], [[Stephen Greenblatt]], [[Jean-Claude Guédon]], [[J. Hillis Miller]], [[Antonio Negri]], [[Peter Suber]] and [[Gayatri Spivak]] among others. ==History== The Open Humanities Press (OHP) is a scholar-led publishing initiative founded by Paul Ashton (Australia), Gary Hall (UK), Sigi Jöttkandt (New Zealand) and David Ottina (US). Its aim is to raise faculty awareness of open access publishing in the humanities and to provide promotional and technical support to open access journals that have been invited by OHP's editorial oversight group to join the collective. OHP launched in May 2008 with 7 open access journals and was named a [http://www.plos.org/cms/comment/reply/219/ "beacon of hope"] by the [[Public Library of Science]]. In August, 2009 OHP announced it will begin publishing open access book series edited by senior members of OHP's board. The first 5 series are: *[http://www.openhumanitiespress.org/new-metaphysics.html New Metaphysics], edited by [[Graham Harman]] and [[Bruno Latour]]; *[http://www.openhumanitiespress.org/critical-climate-change.html Critical Climate Change], edited by Claire Colebrook and Tom Cohen; *[http://www.openhumanitiespress.org/global-conversations.html Global Conversations], edited by [[Ngugi wa Thiong'o]]; *[http://www.openhumanitiespress.org/unidentified-theoretical-objects.html Unidentified Theoretical Objects], edited by [[Wlad Godzich]];
