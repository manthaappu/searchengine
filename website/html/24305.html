{{primary sources|date=December 2009}} Professor '''Michael T. Heath''' (b. 1946 December 11) is a [[computer science|computer scientist]] who specializes in scientific computing. Heath is the Interim Head of the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]] [http://cs.illinois.edu Department of Computer Science]. He is also the director of the [http://www.csar.uiuc.edu/ Center for the Simulation of Advanced Rockets], a [[United States Department of Energy|Department of Energy]]-sponsored computing center and the [http://www.cse.uiuc.edu/ Computational Science and Engineering Program] at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Heath currently holds the Fulton Watson Copp Chair in Computer Science at the University of Illinois. Heath was inducted as a [[Fellow]] of the [[Association for Computing Machinery]] in 2000,[http://fellows.acm.org/ ACM Fellows] and a [[Fellow]] of [[Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics]] in 2010.[http://fellows.siam.org/ SIAM Fellows] He also received the 2009 Taylor L. Booth Education Award from IEEE.[http://www.computer.org/portal/web/csmediacenter/2009-cs-awards IEEE computer society awards] Heath is the author of [http://www.cse.uiuc.edu/heath/scicomp/ ''Scientific Computing: An Introductory Survey''], a popular introductory text on numerical analysis. ==Education== Michael Heath earned his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in [[Mathematics]] from the [[University of Kentucky]] in 1968. In 1974, Heath earned his [[Master of Science|M.S.]] in Mathematics from the [[University of Tennessee]]. Heath earned his [[Ph.D.]] in Computer Science from [[Stanford University]] in 1978. His Ph.D. dissertation was entitled ''[http://oai.dtic.mil/oai/oai?verb=getRecord&metadataPrefix=html&identifier=ADA057962 Numerical Algorithms for Nonlinearly Constrained Optimization]'' and was completed under the direction of [[Gene Golub]]. ==Early work== Prior to his work with the University of Illinois, Michael Heath spent a number of years at [[Oak Ridge National Laboratory]]. Heath joined Oak Ridge in 1968 as a Scientific Applications Programmer, and he became a Eugene P. Wigner Postdoctoral Fellow in 1978. Michael Heath served as an Adjunct Professor of Computer Science at the University of Tennessee from 1988 to 1991.
