{{Recentism|date=September 2010}} '''Equal Justice for Troops blog''' is a military justice and veterans [[blog]] which discusses issues affecting servicemembers and veterans of [[Australia]] and [[United States]]. The topics of the blog concern military justice, health, freedom of information, privacy, defense spending, active duty pay, retirement benefits, housing and national security matters. There is also discussion on the rights of servicemembers and veterans to exercise their rights to redress grievances and issues of fairness affecting military personnel and veterans.[http://justice4troops.blogspot.com/ Equal Justice for Troops blog] (retrieved March 22, 2010) It was created in March 2009 by [[U.S. Navy]] veteran Norbert Basil MacLean III O'Connor Keough who is a [[dual citizen]] of Australia and the United States. In February 2010, the blog was selected for preservation by the [[National Library of Australia]] due to its national significance.[http://pandora.nla.gov.au/tep/115902 National Library of Australia, archived Equal Justice for Troops blog, February 10, 2010] (retrieved March 22, 2010) Besides MacLean (who is known in Australia by his Australian surname of "Keough"), the blog's writers include Nicholas Brian Wiesener who is a law school student at [[University of Technology, Sydney]] and Sean Flynn a [[Queensland]] solicitor.[http://www.qls.com.au/content/ss/solicitor/44945 Queensland Law Society, Flynn, Sean Daniel] (retrieved March 22, 2010) Both Wiesener and Flynn are Australian Army veterans. ==References== {{Reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Equal Justice For Troops Blog}} [[Category:Blogs| Blog]] [[Category:Blogging| Blog]] [[Category:Law blogs]] [[Category:Military justice| ]] {{Internet-publish-stub}}
