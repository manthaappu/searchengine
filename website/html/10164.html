It has two [[Social network service|social networks]] that reach approximately 130,000 people per month.[http://www.quantcast.com/p-62ZEe_qxrsQ92 Quantcast statistics for the Diabetes Hands Foundation websites] The Diabetes Hands Foundation is funded through sponsorship income, donations, grants and earned income.[http://www2.guidestar.org/organizations/26-2274537/diabetes-hands-foundation.aspx Diabetes Hands Foundation Guidestar nonprofit report] == Social media programs == === TuDiabetes and EsTuDiabetes === TuDiabetes and its Spanish counterpart, EsTuDiabetes, are social networking sites for people touched by diabetes and were established in 2007[http://www2.ljworld.com/news/2009/apr/16/social-networking-sites-changing-way-people-learn-/ Social networking sites changing the way people learn about health]. TuDiabetes and EsTuDiabetes have more than 18,000http://www.tudiabetes.org/profiles/members and 12,000http://www.estudiabetes.org/profiles/members registered members respectively. They provide general information, forums and blogs. Both sites are built on the [[Ning (website)|Ning]] platform[http://blog.ning.com/2008/10/nonprofit-know-how-when-networking-on-ning.html Nonprofit know-how when networking on Ning] and are an example of nonprofit online communities[http://mashable.com/2010/02/12/non-profit-communities 5 Tips for Creating Non-Profit Online Communities]. === TuAnalyze === In May 2010, with funding from the [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]], Diabetes Hands Foundation partnered with [[Children's Hospital Boston]] to develop TuAnalyze, a diabetes data mapping tool.[http://www.boston.com/news/health/blog/2010/05/_children_hospi.html Children's project uses social network to map diabetes] Members of TuDiabetes submit their [[Hemoglobin A1C]] data. The service combines the data and displays the results on maps.[http://www.diabetesmine.com/2010/05/introducing-“tuanalyze”-why-mapping-diabetes-data-matters.html Introducing “TuAnalyze”: Why Mapping Diabetes Data Matters] Data entered by participants is stored in the [[Indivo]] [[open-source]] Personally Controlled Health Record ("PCHR") system. The project's goal is to gather information through data donations[http://www.iftf.org/node/3598 What if Self-Tracking Goes Mainstream?] that can be used by researchers and to raise awareness about diabetes. In late 2010, TuAnalyze was made available in Spanish through EsTuDiabetes, under the name EsTuAnálisis. === HealthSeeker === HealthSeeker is a Facebook game that aims to help people live healthy lifestyles[http://www.fastcompany.com/1669932/behavioral-videogames Video Games Modifying Behavior Towards Good][http://blog.games.com/2010/06/21/new-facebook-game-seeks-to-manage-real-world-diabetes-risks/ New Facebook game seeks to manage real-world diabetes risks][http://www.insidefacebook.com/2010/06/21/facebook-app-aims-to-help-control-diabetes/ Facebook App Aims to Help Control Diabetes]. The game was developed in 2010 by the Diabetes Hands Foundation in collaboration with the [[Joslin Diabetes Center]]. Players select missions such as stress-reducing or exercise-inducing activities to complete within a given period of time to help create a healthier lifestyle[http://forecast.diabetes.org/magazine/only-online/facebook-game-focuses-health Facebook Game Focuses on Health][http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KIWWo1y04Z0 Digital Channels for Diabetes: Facebook (video)]. In November 2010, the game was made available in Spanish[http://latino.foxnews.com/latino/health/2010/11/18/diabetes-game-facebook/ Diabetes is a Game -- on Facebook]. == Diabetes awareness programs == {{advert|section|date=September 2010}} The Diabetes Hands Foundation has developed diabetes awareness programs such as Word In Your Hand, the Big Blue Test, No-Sugar Added Poetry and Making Sense of Diabetes. === Word in Your Hand === The Word in Your Hand project invites participants to share photos showing a word in their hands describing life with diabetes. Through the project, people with diabetes along with their family, friends and caregivers describe their feelings towards diabetes.[http://diabeteshandsfoundation.org/Diabetes_Hands_Foundation/Diabetes_Hands_Foundation__|__Our_Work_|__Awareness_Initiatives___|__Word_in_Your_Hand.html Word in Your Hand initiative] DHF licensed the Word in Your Hand trademark to [[LifeScan, Inc.]] and their [[OneTouch]] brand, for use as part of its Global Diabetes Handprint Campaign.[http://www.diabeteshealth.com/read/2008/10/27/5953/lifescan-launches-diabetes-handprint-project-/ LifeScan Launches Diabetes Handprint Project] === Big Blue Test === The Big Blue Test first took place on November 14, 2009[http://forecast.diabetes.org/magazine/only-online/big-blue-test-worldwide-diabetes-test The Big Blue Test: Worldwide Diabetes Test-In], [[World Diabetes Day]]. During the Big Blue Test, at 14:00 hours (local time), people with diabetes test their blood sugar levels, exercise for 14 minutes, test again and share their results. The event is called The Big Blue Test because blue is the color associated with World Diabetes Day. The program aims to both unite people with diabetes and raise awareness about diabetes and the importance of exercise in diabetes management every November 14[http://www.worlddiabetesday.org/en/news/three-months-to-go-to-world-diabetes-day-1 Three months to go to World Diabetes Day!]. In 2010, the Big Blue Test was promoted through a video[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nkLHgK94Z0E Do the BiG Blue Test, on World Diabetes Day] that received more than 120,000 views. === No-Sugar Added Poetry === No-Sugar Added Poetry is a book of diabetes poems[http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/no-sugar-added-poetry-book-tells-diabetes-like-it-is-99754254.html No-Sugar Added Poetry Book Tells Diabetes Like It Is] written by members of TuDiabetes.org with a foreword by William H. Polonsky, founder of the [[Behavioral Diabetes Institute]]. === Making Sense of Diabetes === To raise diabetes awareness and in preparation for World Diabetes Day 2009 on November 14, the foundation held the Making Sense of Diabetes video contest.[http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/booster_shots/2009/09/competition-whats-diabetes-like-to-live-with-make-a-movie.html What's diabetes like to live with? Make a movie.] It sought video entries about life with diabetes through one of the five senses: sight, hearing, taste, touch and smell. It produced a compilation video[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=29bng1H4XTs Making Sense of Diabetes video], showcasing footage from some of the videos for each of the senses. == Awards and Recognition == * TuDiabetes received 2nd place for Niche Social Networking in the 2008 Web 2.0 Awards by SEOmoz.[http://www.seomoz.org/web2.0#cat_89 SEOmoz's Web 2.0 Awards] * In 2009, Diabetes Hands Foundation was designated as a World Diabetes Day Champion by the International Diabetes Federation[http://www.worlddiabetesday.org/en/the-campaign/wdd-champions World Diabetes Day Champions]. * In the 2010 Healthcare Engagement Strategy Awards, TuDiabetes received the Best Patient Community award by Creation Interactive.[http://creationinteractive.com/articles/best-patient-community-award/ Best Patient Community Award - Creation Interactive] * In the 2010 Dose of Digital Dosie Awards, TuDiabetes won 2nd place for Best Patient Community - Non-Brand Controlled (for communities with no direct affiliation to a pharma or healthcare company).[http://www.doseofdigital.com/2010/05/2010-dose-digital-dosie-award-winners/ 2010 Dose of Digital Dosie Award Winners] == References == {{Reflist|2}} == External links == * [http://diabeteshandsfoundation.org/Diabetes_Hands_Foundation/Diabetes_Hands_Foundation.html Diabetes Hands Foundation] * [http://www2.guidestar.org/organizations/26-2274537/diabetes-hands-foundation.aspx Guidestar Nonprofit Report] [[Category:Diabetes organizations]] [[Category:Non-profit organizations]] [[Category:Global internet community]] [[Category:Social network services]] [[Category:Community websites]] [[Category:Virtual communities]] [[Category:Online support groups]] [[Category:Health websites]] [[Category:Health video games]] [[Category:Health informatics]] [[Category:Health advocacy groups]] [[Category:Web 2.0]] [[Category:Social media]] [[Category:Internet properties established in 2007]]
