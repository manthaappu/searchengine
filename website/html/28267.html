In [[computer networking]], a '''packet drop attack''' or '''blackhole attack''' is a type of [[denial-of-service attack]] accomplished by dropping [[packet (information technology)|packet]]s. The attack can be accomplished either selectively (e.g. by dropping packets for a particular network destination, a packet every ''n'' packets or every ''t'' seconds, or a randomly selected portion of the packets, which is called "Gray hole attack") or in bulk (by dropping all packets). ==See also== *[[Black hole (networking)]] ==References== *Raffo, Daniele [http://perso.crans.org/~raffo/papers/phdthesis/thesisch3.html#x10-390003.1.2 Security Schemes for the OLSR Protocol for Ad Hoc Networks], Chapter 3, 3.1.2 Incorrect traffic relaying. {{compu-network-stub}} [[Category:Packets (information technology)]] [[Category:Routing]] [[Category:Denial-of-service attacks]] [[he:שמיטת חבילות מידע]] [[zh:黑洞攻擊]]
