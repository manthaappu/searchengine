The company runs a website, ifixit.com, which sells products for repairing Apple products and publishes guides for taking apart and repairing a variety of products. They are well known for disassembling newly released Apple products for publications such as ''[[PC World (magazine)|PC World]]''[http://www.pcworld.com/businesscenter/article/159852/17inch_macbook_pro_gets_torn_to_pieces.html ''17 inch MacBook Pro gets torn to pieces'', PC World article] and the [[Mac Observer]].[http://www.macobserver.com/article/2008/02/01.6.shtml Mac Observer article, 1 February 2008] On June 4, 2009, iFixit released a platform to allow users to use iFixit's [[product teardown|tear-down]] system to publish their own teardowns.[http://www.ifixit.com/blog/2009/06/new-site-feature-user-submitted-teardowns/ ''Site launch: User-submitted teardowns!'', iFixit.com blog post, 4 June 2009] On December 3, 2009, iFixit released a system called Answers[http://www.ifixit.com/Answers/ Answers page at iFixit.com] for helping users share information about repairing their own hardware.[http://www.ifixit.com/blog/2009/12/ifixit-answers-is-live/ ''iFixit answers is live'', iFixit blog post, 3 December 2009] On [[Earth Day]] (April 22), 2010, iFixit opened their guide-creation software to the public, allowing anyone to publish or edit other repair guides.[http://www.ifixit.com/blog/2010/04/welcome-to-repair-2-0/ ''Welcome to repair 2.0'', iFixit.com blog post, 22 April 2010] ==References== {{reflist}} ==External links== {{official|http://ifixit.com}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Ifixit}} [[Category:Social network services]] [[Category:Community websites]] [[Category:Knowledge markets]] [[Category:Internet properties established in 2003]]
