[[Image:Psion Wavefinder.jpg|right|thumb|upright|The middle of the Psion Wavefinder]] The '''Psion Wavefinder''' was a computer peripheral for receiving [[digital audio broadcasting]] radio signals, made by [[Psion]]. It attached via [[USB]] to a personal computer, and had no loudspeakers or controls of its own, with only a flashing light on the device. Psion hoped it would become a design classic.{{cite news|url=http://www.theregister.co.uk/2000/09/27/psion_mounts_163_299_digital/|title=Psion mounts £299 digital radio land grab|last=Orlowski|first=Andrew|date=27 September 2000|work=The Register|accessdate=2 August 2010}} The Wavefinder was released on 17 October 2000,{{cite news|url=http://www.theregister.co.uk/2001/09/10/psion_wavefinder_saved_by_software/|title=Psion WaveFinder saved by software that works?|last=Lettice|first=John|date=10 September 2001|work=The Register|accessdate=2 August 2010}}{{cite news|url=http://www.pcpro.co.uk/news/17896/psion-launches-pc-digital-radio|title=Psion launches PC digital radio|last=Lock|first=Darren|date=27 September 2000|work=PC Pro|accessdate=2 August 2010}} and gave access to both DAB audio and DAB Data services. The device initially retailed for £299 (at the time the cheapest digital radio on the UK market){{cite news|url=http://www.pcpro.co.uk/reviews/peripherals/818/psion-wavefinder|title=Psion WaveFinder review|last=Lee|first=Fai|date=1 November 2000|work=PC Pro|accessdate=2 August 2010}} and was bundled with new PCs sold by Dixons, but it was quickly discounted, retailing at £49.99 by that December,{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/bristol/content/digitalfuture/2001/12/17/dab.shtml|title=It's a radio revolution!|date=17 December 2001|work=BBC Bristol|accessdate=2 August 2010}} and was no longer produced by 2002.{{cite web|url=http://www.theregister.co.uk/2002/05/15/psion_pulls_plugs_on_modem/|title=Psion pulls plugs on modem business|last=Lettice|first=John|date=15 May 2002|work=The Register|accessdate=2 August 2010}} Psion ended support in 2004. The Wavefinder had frequent software problems, and an unofficial patch called WaveLite was released in 2001. The Wavefinder had widely-reported problems with the USB drivers in [[Windows XP]] Service Pack 2. It will work with [[Windows XP]] with SP3. There are reports that the Wavefinder works in Vista. It is not compatible with [[Macintosh]] computers, except perhaps through the use of a [[PC emulator]] or [[Hardware virtualization|virtualisation]] solution. The unregulated power supply which is supplied with the Wavefinder has an output which can go up to 19 volts, this can cause the Wavefinder to develop faults.
