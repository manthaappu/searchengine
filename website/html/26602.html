[[Entropy]] is considered to be an extensive property, i.e., that its value depends on the amount of material present. [[Constantino Tsallis]] has proposed a '''nonextensive entropy''', which is a generalization of the traditional Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy. The rationale behind the theory is that Gibbs-Boltzmann entropy leads to systems that have a strong dependence on initial conditions. In reality most materials behave quite independently of initial conditions.
