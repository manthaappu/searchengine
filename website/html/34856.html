{{Infobox Non-profit | Non-profit_name = Society of Manufacturing Engineers | Non-profit_logo = [[Image:Optimized image abfba013.png|200px]] | Non-profit_type = | founded_date = 1932 | location = [[Image:Flag of the United States.svg|20px]] [[Dearborn, Michigan]], [[USA]] | | homepage = http://www.sme.org }} Through its many programs and activities, the Society of Manufacturing Engineers (SME) connects manufacturing practitioners to each other, to the latest technology and the most up-to-date processes spanning all manufacturing industries and disciplines, plus the key areas of aerospace and defense, medical device, motor vehicles, including motorsports, oil and gas, and alternative energy. ==History of the Society== Founded in 1932 with 33 members, the organization was originally named The Society of Tool Engineers (STE). A year later, it was renamed the American Society of Tool Engineers (ASTE). It finally became the Society of Manufacturing Engineers in 1969. ==Membership== SME [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/getsmepg.pl?/html/chap_region.htm&&&SME& local chapters] and [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/getsmepg.pl?/communities/tcn.htm&&&SME& technical communities] are real-life and virtual venues where members and other manufacturing practitioners can connect and interact, to exchange ideas and information from one peer to another. ==Events== SME's [http://www.biztradeshows.com/organizers/society-manufacturing-engineers.html industry-leading events] keep manufacturers connected — to each other and to the latest manufacturing equipment, production methods and management strategies. ==Publications== SME publishes [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/getsmepg.pl?/gmn/mag/home.html&&&SME&NONAV& Manufacturing Engineering magazine] and [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/getsmepg.pl?/html/yearbooks.htm&&&SME& industry-related yearbook series], several hundred educational [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/getgmnpage.pl?/html/storehome.html&&&SME& books and videos], two [http://www.sciencedirect.com/sme peer-reviewed scholarly journals] and more than 17,000 [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/getsmepg.pl?/html/techpapers.htm&&SME& technical papers]. ==Professional Development== SME's professional development opportunities, available to individuals, companies and educators, provide the right tools and information to help advance careers and drive operations to new levels of productivity and competitiveness. SME offers several industry-standard certifications for professional recognition, and documentation of a member’s manufacturing-related knowledge and skills. These include the [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/certhtml.pl?/cert/cmfgtp.htm&&SME& Certified Manufacturing Technologist (CMfgT)], [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/certhtml.pl?/cert/cmfgep.htm&&SME& Certified Manufacturing Engineering (CMfgE) Certification] and [http://www.sme.org/cgi-bin/certhtml.pl?/cert/lean_certification.htm&&SME& Lean Certification].
