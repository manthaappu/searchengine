[[Image:Odra 1305 jednostka centralna.jpg|250px|right|thumb|Odra No. 1305, [[1974]]]] '''Odra''' was a line of [[computer]]s manufactured in [[Wrocław]], [[Poland]]. The production started in 1959–1960. Models 1001, 1002, 1003, 1013, 1103, 1204 were of original Polish construction. Models 1304 and 1305 were functional counterparts of [[International Computers Limited|ICL]] 1905 and 1906 due to software agreement.http://itpedia.pl/index.php/ODRA_1305 The last model was 1325 based on two models from ICL. The name comes from the [[Oder river|Odra River]] that flows through the city of Wrocław The computers were built at the [[Elwro]] manufacturing plant, which was closed in 1989. ODRA-1002 were capable of only 100-400 operations per second.http://www.warmus.com.pl/pdf/tytul_ang.pdf The operating system used by the ODRA-1204 is called SODA. It was designed to work on a small computer without magnetic storage and can run simultaneous loading and execution of programs.http://comjnl.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/content/abstract/11/2/148 An [[ALGOL 68]] compiler was produced for the ODRA 1204/IL in 1976. As for 2008 there is still at least one machine working at railway station in Wrocław Brochów.{{cite web |url=http://www.wfp.pl/wroclaw-miasto/news.php?id=10004|title=Zabytkowa Odra pracuje na stacji kolejowej|opublikowany=Alert24.pl|date=25 April 2008|accessdate=26 April 2008|language=pl|author=Jacek Bomersbach |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080530115600/http://www.wfp.pl/wroclaw-miasto/news.php?id=10004 |archivedate = 30 May 2008}} This machine was shut down on 30 April 2010, 22:00 CEST{{cite web | title=Wyłączono ostatni komputer Odra w Polsce! | url=http://www.computerworld.pl/news/358487/Wylaczono.ostatni.komputer.Odra.w.Polsce.html}} and replaced with a contemporary computer system. Few Odras are placed on exhibition in museums and at least one is still operational and maintained by students of electrical school which created a fan club around the machine.
