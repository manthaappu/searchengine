{{Infobox Company | company_name = Lumeta Corporation| company_type = [[Private company|Private]]| company_slogan = Global Network Visibility| foundation = 2000| location_city = [[Somerset, New Jersey|Somerset]], [[New Jersey]] | location_country = [[United States|USA]] | industry = [[Computer software]], [[Computer hardware]], [[Computer network security]], [[Network mapping]], [[network discovery software]]| homepage = [http://www.lumeta.com/ www.lumeta.com] }} '''Lumeta Corporation''' is a [[privately-held company]] that is headquartered in [[Somerset, New Jersey]],[http://www.njbiz.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=81188:headline-expanding-lumeta-moves-to-a-bigger-nj-home&catid=Daily%20News:&Itemid=2] which develops [[network mapping]] software, [[network discovery software]] and network leak detection software.[http://itknowledgeexchange.techtarget.com/it-compliance/network-mapping-and-leak-detection-at-disa] == Corporate History == The first generation of Lumeta's technology was developed in 1998 by [[Bell Labs]] scientists, and is known as the [[Internet Mapping Project]]. [http://www.cheswick.com/ches/map] [http://blogs.wsj.com/biztech/2008/06/10/a-look-at-the-internet-then-and-now/] Lumeta spun out from Bell Labs in 2000 and used the original technology as a basis for creating the company's product IPsonar. [http://www.eweek.com/c/a/IT-Infrastructure/Discovery-Tool-Helps-Firm-See-Whole-Network/] == Products == Lumeta IPsonar is a [[network mapping]], network leak detection and [[network discovery software]] [http://www.defensenews.com/story.php?i=4458190]. The product gathers information about the network including discovery of all [[Internet Protocol|IP]]-connected devices on a network, and how they are interconnected, locating previously unknown [[IP addresses]] and gathering information about the devices and services on the network. [http://gcn.com/articles/2008/09/14/network-in-full-view.aspx] IPsonar’s Leak Discovery tests devices on the network for inbound and outbound connectivity. [http://gcn.com/Articles/2005/12/08/Program-detects-dangers-that-run-silent-run-deep.aspx] The product also has the capability to discover rogue [[wireless access points]].
