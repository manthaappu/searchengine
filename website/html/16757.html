Yamada''' (? - May 21, 2008, age 77) was a Japanese [[computer scientist]], known for his influential contributions to [[theoretical computer science]], as well as for the development of Japanese keyboard layouts, a challenging practical problem. From 1972 to 1991, he was professor of the [[formal languages]] division at the Department for Information Science at the [[University of Tokyo]]. ==Work== In the field of theoretical computer science, Yamada introduced the notion of real-time computability. As his colleague [[Aravind Joshi]] recalls: {{quote|This work was very positively reviewed not only in the computer science related journals but also in other theoretical journals such as the Journal of Symbolic Logic.
