Professor '''Simon M. Kirby''' is a British [[computational linguist]], currently holding the Chair of Language Evolution at the [[University of Edinburgh]], where he is Director of the Graduate School, and Programme Director for the MSc in the Evolution of Language and Cognition. He specializes in [[evolutionary]] computational models of human [[language]] and its development. He is a creator, together with members of the experimental pop band [[Found|FOUND]], of [[Cybraphon]], an "autonomous emotional robot band" in a wardrobe,{{Cite web | title = Cybraphon | url = http://cybraphon.com/ | accessdate = 19 November 2009}} which won a [[BAFTA]] in 2009.{{Cite web | title = FOUND win BAFTA for Cybraphon | date = 9 November 2009 | url = http://foundtheband.com/main-feature/found-win-bafta-for-cybraphon/ | accessdate = 19 November 2009}} ==Publications== Kirby has published over 52 articles, chapters, and other publications, including the books: *Christiansen, M. and Kirby, S.
