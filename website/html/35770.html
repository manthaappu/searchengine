{{About|the computer scientist|the con artist|Steven Jay Russell}} {{BLP sources|date=April 2009}} {{Infobox scientist | name = Stephen Russell | image = Steve Russell-PDP-1-20070512.jpg | image_width = | caption = | birth_date = 1937 | birth_place = | death_date = | death_place = | residence = [[Image:Flag of the United States.svg|20px|]] [[United States|U.S.]] | field = [[Computer science]] | work_institution = | alma_mater = [[Dartmouth College]] | known_for = [[Spacewar!]], [[Lisp (programming language)|Lisp]] | spouse = }} [[Image:Spacewar!-PDP-1-20070512.jpg|thumb|left|[[Spacewar!]] on the [[Computer History Museum]]'s [[PDP-1]], 2007]] '''Steve "Slug" Russell''' is a programmer and computer scientist most famous for creating [[Spacewar!]], one of the earliest [[videogame]]s, in 1961 with the fellow members of the [[Tech Model Railroad Club]] at [[MIT]] working on a [[Digital Equipment Corporation|DEC]] Digital PDP-1.{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2002/02/28/technology/a-long-time-ago-in-a-lab-far-away.html|title=A Long Time Ago, in a Lab Far Away . . .|last=Markoff|first=John|date=2002-02-28|publisher=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=2009-04-27}} While there is some debate over priority regarding the concept of computer-based games in general, Spacewar! was unquestionably the first to gain widespread recognition, and is generally recognized as the first of the "shoot-'em' up" genre. Steve Russell wrote the first two implementations of [[Lisp (programming language)|Lisp]] for the [[IBM 704]]. It was Russell who realized that the concept of [[universal function]]s could be applied to the language; by implementing the Lisp universal evaluator in a lower-level language, it became possible to create the Lisp [[Interpreter (computing)|interpreter]] (previous development work on the language had focused on [[Compiler|compiling]] the language).{{cite web | url = http://www-formal.stanford.edu/jmc/history/lisp.ps | title = History of Lisp | author = John McCarthy}} He invented the [[continuation]] to solve a double recursion problem for one of the users of his Lisp implementation.{{cite web | url = http://www.computernostalgia.net/articles/steveRussell.htm | title = Steve "Slug" Russell | work = Computer History}} Steve Russell was educated at [[Dartmouth College]] from 1954 to 1958. ==References== {{Reflist}} ==External links== {{Commons category|Steve Russell}} * [http://www.thedoteaters.com/stage1.php The Dot Eaters entry] on Russell and the development of Spacewar == See also == *[[First video game]] {{Persondata | NAME = Russell, Steve | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = S.
