'''James Tice Ellis''' (6 May 1956 – 28 June 2001) was a [[computer scientist]] best known as the co-creator of [[Usenet]], along with [[Tom Truscott]]. Born in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], Ellis grew up in [[Orlando, Florida]]. Before developing Usenet, Ellis attended [[Duke University]]. He later worked as an Internet security consultant for [[Sun Microsystems]]. He was also Manager of Technical Development at [[CERT_Coordination_Center|CERT]]. He was married and had two children when he died of [[lymphoma]] in 2001 in [[Harmony, Pennsylvania]]. He came up with the word ''Usenet''.
