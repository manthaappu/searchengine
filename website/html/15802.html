{{Primary|date=June 2010}} {{Infobox Software | name = Greenstone | screenshot = [[Image:GLI-2.74.png|250px]] | caption = Greenstone's Graphical Librarian Interface (GLI) v2.80 | developer = [[University of Waikato]] | latest_release_version = Greenstone2: 2.83, Greenstone3: 3.02 | operating_system = [[Cross-platform]] | genre = [[Digital library|Digital libraries ]] | license = [[GNU General Public License|GPL]] | website = [http://www.greenstone.org/ www.greenstone.org] | }} '''Greenstone''' is a suite of [[software]] tools for building and distributing [[digital library]] collections on the [[Internet]] or [[CD-ROM]]. It is [[open source|open-source]], multilingual software, issued under the terms of the [[GNU General Public License]]. Greenstone is produced by the New Zealand Digital Library Project at the [[University of Waikato]], and has been developed and distributed in cooperation with [[UNESCO]] and the Human Info NGO in Belgium. K.S. Raghavan, A. Neelameghan and S. K. Lalitha. 2010. Co-creation and development of digital library software. ''Information Studies'' 16(2):65–72.Michael Lesk. 2004. ''Understanding digital libraries.'' Second edition. San Francisco: Morgan Kaufmann Publishers, p. 171-172. The developers of Greenstone received the [[International Federation for Information Processing]]'s 2004 Namur Award for "contributions to the awareness of social implications of information technology, and the need for an holistic approach in the use of information technology that takes account of social implications."{{Cite web |url=http://www.info.fundp.ac.be/~jbl/IFIP/award.html |title=IFIP-WG9.2 Namur Award |accessdate=30 September 2010 |work=IFIP-WG9.2 Namur Award |publisher=Prof. Jacques Berleur Facultés Universitaires Notre-Dame de la Paix}} Greenstone may be used to create large, searchable collections of digital documents. In addition to command line tools for digital collection building, Greenstone has a graphical Greenstone Librarians Interface (GLI) used to build collections and assign metadata. Zhang, Alison B. and Don Gourley. 2008. ''Creating digital collections: a practical guide''. Oxford: Chandos Publishing, p. 176-177. Through user selected plugins, Greenstone can import digital documents in formats including text, html, jpg, tiff, MP3, PDF, video, and Word, among others. The text, PDF, HTML and similar documents are converted into Greenstone Archive Format (GAF) which is an XML equivalent format. Witten, Ian H. and David Bainbridge. 2003. ''How to build a digital library''. San Francisco: Morgan Kaufmann Publishers, p. 313-323.
