'''Macaulay2''' is a [[Free software|free]] [[computer algebra system]] developed by Daniel Grayson (UIUC) and Michael Stillman (Cornell) for computation in [[commutative algebra]] and [[algebraic geometry]]. Stillman, along with [[Dave Bayer]] had authored the predecessor, [[Macaulay computer algebra system|Macaulay]]. Macaulay2 uses its own [[high level programming language]], intended to closely match the syntax used by [[mathematician]]s in the field. Both are published under the [[GNU General Public License]] version 2.
