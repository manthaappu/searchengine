'''''Index Medicus''''' ('''IM''') is a comprehensive index of [[Medicine|medical]] [[scientific journal]] articles, published since 1879. It was initiated by [[John Shaw Billings]], head of the [[Library of the Surgeon General's Office]], [[United States Army]]. This library later evolved into the [[United States National Library of Medicine]] (NLM), which continues publication of the ''Index''. == History == Publication began in 1879 and continued monthly through 1926, with a hiatus between 1899 and 1902.{{cite web |url=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/services/indexmedicus.html |title=FAQ: Index Medicus Chronology |format= |work= |accessdate=2009-09-06}}{{cite web |url=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/hmd/about/collectionhistory.html |title=History of Historical Collections - History of Medicine - National Library of Medicine |format= |work= |accessdate=2009-09-06}} During this hiatus, a similar index, the ''Bibliographia medica'', was published in [[French language|French]] by the Institut de Bibliographie in [[Paris]]. The ''Index Medicus'' was amalgamated with the [[American Medical Association]]'s ''Quarterly Cumulative Index to Current Literature'' (QCICL) as the ''Quarterly Cumulative Index Medicus'' (QCIM) in 1927 and the AMA continued to publish this until 1956. From 1960 to 2004 the printed edition was published by the National Library of Medicine under the name ''Index Medicus/Cumulated Index Medicus'' (IM/CIM). An abridged version was published from 1970 to 1997 as the ''Abridged Index Medicus''.{{cite web |url=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/news/aim.html |title=Abridged Index Medicus Ceasing Publication |format= |work= |accessdate=2009-09-06}} The abridged edition lives on as a subset of the journals covered by [[PubMed]] ("core clinical journals").{{cite web |url=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/services/aim.html |title=FAQ: Finding a List of Abridged Index Medicus or Index Medicus Journal Titles |format= |work= |accessdate=2009-09-06}} The last issue of ''Index Medicus'' was published in December 2004 (Volume 45). The stated reason for discontinuing the printed publication was that online resources had supplanted it,{{cite web | title = 'Index Medicus'' - NLM Technical Bulletin to Cease as Print Publication | publisher = [[United States National Library of Medicine|National Library of Medicine]] - NLM Technical Bulletin | date = 2004-05-04 | url = http://www.nlm.nih.gov/pubs/techbull/mj04/mj04_im.html | accessdate=2008-04-16}} most especially PubMed, which continues to include the ''Index'' as a subset of the journals it covers.{{cite web |url=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/bsd/num_titles.html |title=Number of Titles Currently Indexed for Index Medicus and MEDLINE on PubMed |format= |work= |accessdate=2009-09-06}} == Journal selection == Inclusion into the ''Index'' is not automatic and depends on a journal's scientific policy and scientific quality.{{cite web |url=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/bsd/index_4_medline/tsld003.htm |title=How are journals selected for Index Medicus and MEDLINE? |format= |work= |accessdate=2009-09-06}} These criteria are evaluated by the "Literature Selection Technical Review Committee" and the final decision is made by the NLM director.
