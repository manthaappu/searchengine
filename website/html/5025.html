{{Wikify|date=December 2010}} {{orphan|date=August 2009}} '''CFD-DEM''' model is suitable for the modelling or simulation of [[fluid]]-solids or fluid-particles systems. In the CFD-DEM, the motion of discrete solids or particles phase is obtained by the Discrete Element Method (DEM) which applies Newton's laws of motion to every particle, while the flow of continuum fluid is described by the local averaged Navierâ€“Stokes equations that can be solved using the traditional Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) approach. The interactions between the fluid phase and solids phase is modeled by use of [[Newton's third law]].
