{{Infobox One-of-a-kind computers |Image= [[File:JaguarXT5.jpg|frameless]] |Caption= ''Jaguar'', a Cray XT5 supercomputer at [[National Center for Computational Sciences]] at [[Oak Ridge National Laboratories]] |Website= |Dates= November 6, 2007 |Location= |Sponsors= |Operators= |Architecture= |Memory= |Storage= |Speed= |Power= |Space= |Cost= |ChartName=TOP500 |ChartPosition= |ChartDate= |Purpose= |Legacy= |Emulators= |Sources=[http://www.top500.org/system/10186 top500.org] |Website= }} [[File:Oak Ridge - Kraken (Cray XT5).JPG|thumb|''Kraken'', a Cray XT5 supercomputer at [[National Institute for Computational Sciences]] at [[Oak Ridge National Laboratories]]]] The '''[[Cray]] XT5''' is an updated version of the [[Cray XT4]] [[supercomputer]], launched on November 6, 2007. It includes a faster version of the XT4's SeaStar2 interconnect router called [[PowerPC 400#SeaStar|SeaStar2+]], and can be configured either with XT4 compute blades, which have four [[dual-core]] [[AMD Opteron]] processor sockets, or XT5 blades, with eight sockets supporting dual or quad-core Opterons. The XT5 uses a 3-dimensional torus network topology. The XT5 family run the [[Cray Linux Environment]], formerly known as [[UNICOS]]/lc.{{cite book|title=Cray XT System Software 2.1 Release Overview (S–2425–21)|publisher=[[Cray|Cray Inc]]|date=2008|url=http://www.nersc.gov/vendor_docs/cray/S-2425-21-OS2.1-Release-Overview.pdf}} This incorporates [[SUSE Linux Enterprise Server]] and Cray's [[Compute Node Linux]]. The '''XT5h''' (''hybrid'') variant also includes support for [[Cray X2]] [[vector processor]] blades, and Cray XR1 blades which combine Opterons with [[FPGA]]-based Reconfigurable Processor Units (RPUs) provided by DRC Computer Corporation. The '''XT5m''' variant is a mid-ranged supercomputer with most of the features of the XT5, but having a 2-dimensional torus network topology and scalable to 6 cabinets. In the fall of 2008, Cray delivered a 1.3 petaflops XT5 system to [[National Center for Computational Sciences]] at [[Oak Ridge National Laboratories]]. This system, with over 150,000 processing cores, was dubbed "[[Jaguar (computer)|Jaguar]]" and was the second fastest system in the world for the [[LINPACK]] benchmark,[http://www.top500.org/lists/2008/11 TOP500 November 2008] the fastest system available for open science and the first system to exceed a [[FLOPS|petaflops]] sustained performance on a 64-bit scientific application.[http://www.nccs.gov/jaguar/ National Center for Computational Sciences: Jaguar] Jaguar underwent an upgrade to 224,256 cores in 2009, after which its performance jumped to 1.75 petaflops, taking it to the number one position in the 34th edition of the [[TOP500]] list in fall 2009.[http://www.top500.org/lists/2009/11 TOP500 November 2009] It remained number one in the June 2010 edition,[http://www.top500.org/lists/2010/06 TOP500 June 2010] but in October 2010 was surpassed by the Chinese [[Tianhe-1A]], which achieved a performance of 2.57 petaflops.{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/technology-11644252|title=China claims supercomputer crown|publisher=BBC News Online|date=28 October 2010|accessdate=29 October 2010}} Another XT5 system, Kraken, with 99,072 cores and 0.83 petaflops, is currently at position number eight in the [[TOP500]] list as of November 2010. ==References== ==External links== {{commonscat}} * [http://www.cray.com/products/XT.aspx Cray XT5 product page] * [http://investors.cray.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=98390&p=irol-newsArticle&ID=1073071 Cray Introduces Next-Generation Supercomputers] * [http://www.cray.com/Products/XT5/Product/Software.aspx Cray Linux Environment] {{Cray computers}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Cray Xt5}} [[Category:Supercomputers]] {{super-compu-stub}} [[ar:كراي XT5]] [[es:Cray XT-5]] [[it:Cray XT5]]
