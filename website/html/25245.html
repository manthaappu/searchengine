{{Orphan|date=January 2011}} '''Multi-configuration time-dependent [[Douglas Hartree|Hartree]]''' (MCTDH) is a general algorithm to solve the time-dependent [[Schrödinger equation]] for multidimensional dynamical systems consisting of distinguishable [[Elementary particle|particles]]. MCTDH can thus determine the quantal motion of the nuclei of a [[molecule|molecular system]] evolving on one or several [[vibronic coupling|coupled]] electronic [[potential energy surface]]s. MCTDH by its very nature is an approximate method. However, it can be made as accurate as any competing method, but its numerical efficiency deteriorates with growing accuracy. MCTDH is designed for multi-dimensional problems, in particular for problems that are difficult or even impossible to attack in a conventional way. There is no or only little gain when treating systems with less than three [[degrees of freedom (physics and chemistry)|degrees of freedom]] by MCTDH. MCTDH will in general be best suited for systems with 4 to 12 degrees of freedom. Because of hardware limitations it may in general not be possible to treat much larger systems. For a certain class of problems, however, one can go much further. The MCTDH program package has recently been generalised to enable the propagation of [[density operator]]s. ==References== H. D.
