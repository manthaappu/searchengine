In [[mathematics]], a '''semigroup with two elements''' is a [[semigroup]] for which the [[cardinality]] of the [[underlying set]] is [[two]]. There are exactly five [[distinct]] [[isomorphic|nonisomorphic]] semigroups having two elements: * ''O''2, the [[null semigroup]] of order two, * ''LO''2 and ''RO''2, the [[left zero semigroup]] of order two and [[right zero semigroup]] of order two, respectively, * ({0,1}, ∧) (where "∧" is the [[logical connective]] "[[Logical conjunction|and]]"), the non-null semigroup with [[Null semigroup#Zero element in a semigroup|zero]] of order two, * (''Z''2, +2) (where ''Z''2 = {0,1} and "+2" is "[[Modular addition|addition modulo 2]]"): the [[group (mathematics)|group]] of order two. The semigroups ''LO''2 and ''RO''2 are [[antiisomorphic]]. ''O''2, {{nowrap|({0,1}, ∧)}} and {{nowrap|(''Z''2, +2)}} are [[commutativity|commutative]], ''LO''2 and ''RO''2 are noncommutative. ''LO''2, ''RO''2 and {{nowrap|({0,1}, ∧)}} are [[band (algebra)|band]]s and also inverse semigroups. ==Determination of semigroups with two elements== Choosing the set {{nowrap begin}}''A'' = { 1, 2 }{{nowrap end}} as the underlying set having two elements, sixteen [[binary operation]]s can be defined in ''A''. {| class="wikitable" border="1" |- ! \wedge ! 0 ! 1 |- | '''0''' | 0 | 0 |- | '''1''' | 0 | 1 |}
This is the simplest non-trivial example of a semigroup that is not a group. This semigroup is commutative and has an identity element, which is 1 . It is not a group because the element 0 does not have an inverse. In fact, it is not even a cancellative semigroup because we cannot cancel the 0 in the equation 1·0 = 0·0. This semigroup arises in various contexts. For instance, if we choose 1 to be the [[truth value]] "[[true]]" and 0 to be the [[truth value]] "[[false]]" and the operation to be the [[logical connective]] "[[Logical conjunction|and]]", we obtain this semigroup in [[logic]]. It is also isomorphic to the semigroup : S = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \right\} under [[matrix multiplication]].{{planetmath|id=8498|title=Semigroup with two elements}} ==The two-element semigroup (''Z''2,+2)== The [[Cayley table]] for the semigroup (''Z''2,+2) is given below:

This group is isomorphic to the [[symmetric group]] ''S''2. ==Finite semigroups of higher orders== Let ''A'' be the three-element set { 1, 2, 3 }. Altogether, a total of 39 = 19683 different binary operations can be defined on ''A''. It is impractical to prepare a list of all these binary operations, and determine those that make ''A'' nonisomorphic semigroups. Algorithms and computer programs have been developed for determining nonisomorphic finite semigroups of a given order.
