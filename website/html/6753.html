{{Orphan|date=November 2007}} '''Cognitive description''' is a term used in psychology to describe the cognitive workings of the human mind. A cognitive description specifies what kinds of information is input to a cognitive action, how this information is processed and transformed, what data structures are used and what behaviour is generated.[[John Tooby|Tooby, John]] and [[Leda Cosmides|Cosmides, Leda]] 1992 ''The Psychological Foundations of Culture'', in Barkow, Jerome H., Cosmides, Leda, Tooby, John, (1992) ''[[The Adapted Mind]]: Evolutionary Psychology and the Generation of Culture,'' Oxford University Press, ISBN 0-19-506023-7, page 64. ==See also== {{Portal|Thinking}} [[Cognitive module]] ==References== {{Reflist}} [[Category:Behavioural sciences]] [[Category:Cognition]] [[Category:Cognitive science]] [[Category:Evolutionary psychology]] [[Category:Ethology]] [[Category:Linguistics]] [[Category:Philosophy of mind]] [[Category:Semantics]] {{Philosophy-stub}}
