Users '[[Check-in#Social check-in|check in]]' at ''Spots'' in their local vicinity, either through a dedicated mobile application or through the mobile website. As a reward users will sometimes receive items from check-ins. Items have developed to become a promotional tool for the game's partners, e.g. [[Incase Designs|Incase]]. This is achieved either through the use of dedicated applications available on Google [[Android (operating system)|Android]], [[iPhone]], Palm [[WebOS]] and [[BlackBerry]], or via ''m.gowalla.com''. There is currently no way of checking-in through the standard website. Check-ins can be pushed via Notifications to iPhones, and by linking accounts, to [[Twitter]] and [[Facebook]]. Trips, which as of January 2010 {{cite web |url=http://gowalla.com/blog/2010/01/trips-and-bookmarks |title=New Features: Trips and Bookmarks |author= |date=2010-01-28 |work=Gowalla Blog |publisher= |accessdate=18 March 2010}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}} can be made by any user, link up to 20 related spots, falling into categories such as Nature Hikes or Pub Crawls. Spots and Trips can be ''featured'' by Gowalla, and with this comes a special status and icon, along with being highlighted on the website. Featured spots tend to be local landmarks such as [[Buckingham Palace]] in London, whilst featured Trips are chosen for being 'unique and exciting'. Upon checking-in, users may receive items as a bonus and these can be swapped or dropped at Spots. Users become ''Founders'' of a spot by dropping an item there. Items form a key feature within the game and each user has a vault into which they can place items they want to keep forever. Special items have been created for specific events of importance such as the [[2010 Winter Olympics|2010 Winter Olympic Games]], and also as promotional items linked to real-world prizes. On December 2, 2010, Gowalla released version 3.0 for the iPhone 4, allowing Gowalla users to check-in using Foursquare, Facebook Places, Twitter and Tumblr, or view friends's check-ins from other services.{{cite news | first=David | last=Chartier | title=Gowalla 3.0 Unites Facebook, Foursquare Check-ins | url=http://www.pcworld.com/businesscenter/article/212251/gowalla_30_unites_facebook_foursquare_checkins.html | accessdate=2 December 2010}} ==Street Team Elite== Within the Gowalla community, certain users have an elevated status above that of normal users.
