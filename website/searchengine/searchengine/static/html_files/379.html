'''ALCOR''' is an early computer language definition created by the '''ALCOR Group''', a consortium of universitites, research institutions and manufacturers in Europe and the USA which was founded in 1959 and which had 60 members in 1966. The group had the aim of a common [[compiler]] specification for a subset of [[ALGOL 60]] after the ALGOL meeting in Copenhagen in 1958. ALCOR is an acronym, from '''AL'''GOL '''Co'''nverte'''r'''. In Europe, a high level machine architecture for [[ALGOL 60]] was devised which was emulated on various real computers, among them the Siemens 2002 and the [[IBM 7090]].
